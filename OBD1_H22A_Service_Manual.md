## Engine (H22A1)

### Torque Specifications

#### Cylinder Head Bolts (10 x 1.25 mm)
**Three-Step Torque Procedure:**

| Step | Torque |
|------|--------|
| **Step 1** | 29 ft-lb (39 N·m) |
| **Step 2** | 51 ft-lb (69 N·m) |
| **Step 3** | 72.3 ft-lb (98 N·m) |

**Torque Sequence:**
```
Intake side:  9 - 5 - 1 - 3 - 7
Exhaust side: 8 - 4 - 2 - 6 - 10
```
*Apply clean engine oil to threads and under nuts/washers*

**Removal Sequence:**
```
Intake side:  2 - 6 - 10 - 8 - 4
Exhaust side: 3 - 7 - 9 - 5 - 1
```

#### Main Bearing Caps (11 x 1.5 mm)
- **Torque:** 54 ft-lb (73 N·m)

### Engine Removal/Installation

#### Removal Procedure
1. Disconnect battery negative cable
2. Drain coolant and engine oil
3. Remove air cleaner assembly
4. Disconnect fuel lines (relieve pressure first)
5. Disconnect all electrical connectors
6. Remove intake and exhaust manifolds
7. Disconnect shift cables (manual) or cable bracket (automatic)
8. Support engine with hoist
9. Remove engine mounts
10. Lower engine carefully

#### Installation Notes
- Use new gaskets and O-rings
- Torque all bolts to specification
- Refill with correct fluids
- Check for leaks after start-up

### VTEC System

#### VTEC Operation
- **Engagement RPM:** ~5,500 RPM
- **Oil pressure required:** 30+ psi
- **Control:** ECU activates solenoid based on RPM, load, and temperature

#### VTEC Solenoid
- **Location:** Top of cylinder head
- **Resistance:** 14-30 ohms
- **Error code:** P1259 (VTEC system malfunction)

#### VTEC Troubleshooting
1. Check oil level and pressure
2. Test solenoid resistance
3. Check wiring and grounds
4. Inspect solenoid screen for debris
5. Verify oil pressure switch operation

## Fuel & Emissions

### Fuel System

#### Fuel Pump
- **Type:** Electric, in-tank
- **Flow rate:** 150 L/hr min
- **Pressure:** 33-40 psi (H22A1)

#### Fuel Injectors
- **Type:** Sequential multi-port
- **Resistance:** 11-15 ohms (cold)
- **Flow rate:** 24 lb/hr (stock)

### Emissions Control

#### EVAP System
- **Canister:** Activated carbon
- **Purge control:** Solenoid valve
- **Vent shut valve:** Solenoid operated

#### EGR System
- **Type:** Vacuum-operated
- **Control:** ECU via solenoid
- **Lift sensor:** Integrated

#### Secondary Air Injection
- **Type:** Air pump (early models)
- **Control:** Vacuum-operated valve

## SRS System

### Supplemental Restraint System (SRS)

#### Warning Labels
- **CAUTION:** Always disconnect battery before SRS work
- **WARNING:** Airbags can cause serious injury if deployed improperly
- **NOTE:** Radio may have theft protection code

#### SRS Components
- Driver's airbag
- Front passenger's airbag
- SRS unit (control module)
- Dash sensors (left and right)
- Cable reel (clockspring)
- Warning indicator

#### SRS Service Precautions

**Before Service:**
1. Disconnect battery negative cable first
2. Wait at least 3 minutes for capacitor discharge
3. Install short connector (RED) on airbag connectors
4. Get radio security code

**During Service:**
- Never use used SRS parts
- Inspect all components for damage
- Do not disassemble airbag or SRS unit
- Keep short connectors installed when harness disconnected

**After Service:**
1. Remove short connectors
2. Reconnect all airbag connectors
3. Reconnect battery (positive first, then negative)
4. Verify SRS light operation (on for 6 seconds, then off)
5. Enter radio code

#### SRS Airbag Deployment Procedure

**In-Car Deployment:**
1. Disconnect both battery cables
2. Confirm airbag is securely mounted
3. Remove access panel/glove box
4. Disconnect airbag connector
5. Strip wires and connect deployment tool
6. Position tool 30 feet away
7. Connect 12V battery to tool
8. Push deployment switch
9. Wait 30 minutes before handling
10. Dispose in sealed plastic bag

**Out-of-Car Deployment:**
1. Position airbag face up on flat ground
2. Keep 30 feet from obstacles/people
3. Follow same procedure as in-car
4. Wear face shield and gloves
5. Wash hands after handling

#### SRS Troubleshooting

**Common Issues:**
- **Light stays on:** Check for codes, verify grounds
- **Light doesn't come on:** Check indicator circuit
- **No deployment:** Check igniter circuit, battery voltage

**Diagnostic Steps:**
1. Read DTCs with scan tool
2. Check SRS unit grounds
3. Verify wiring continuity
4. Test components with special tools
5. Replace faulty components (never repair)

## Additional Reference Sections

### Section 23-320: Airbag Assembly Replacement
- Torque: 10 N·m (7.2 lb-ft) for TORX bolts
- Replace all bolts (do not reuse)
- Check horn and cruise control after installation

### Section 23-325: Cable Reel Replacement
- Align cancel sleeve grooves with cable reel projections
- Center cable reel before installing steering wheel
- Verify yellow gear tooth alignment

### Section 23-329: SRS Unit Replacement
- Torque: 10 N·m (7.2 lb-ft) for 6mm TORX bolts
- Push connector until it clicks
- Verify system operation after installation
