**Credibility scale:**
- **A+:** OEM service manuals, official Honda documentation, manufacturer specs
- **A:** Industry-standard technical publications, specialist performance sites (EngineLabs, KStuned)
- **B+:** Reputable automotive media (Car and Driver, MotorTrend, Road & Track)
- **B:** Wikipedia, enthusiast forums (Honda-Tech, PreludePower), specification databases
- **C:** Social media, personal blogs, eBay listings, unverified sources
- **D:** Rumor, speculation, unattributed claims

## Notes

### Conflicts & Uncertainties

List any discrepancies between sources, areas where consensus is lacking, or claims that couldn't be independently verified. Be specific about:
- What the conflict is
- Which sources take which position
- Why there may be a discrepancy
- How you resolved it (or why it remains unresolved)

### Key Takeaways

Bullet points of the most important conclusions. These are the facts the next operator (or the user) should remember.

### Implications for Building/Modding

Practical guidance derived from the research. When applicable, include:
- Recommended upgrade paths by budget/stage
- Known failure modes and prevention
- Parts availability and pricing
- Swap compatibility notes

## Citations

Full citation format for all sources. Include:
1. Author/organization
2. Title
3. Publication/platform
4. Year (if available)
5. URL
6. Retrieved date
7. Credibility rating
8. Access method (tavily_search, tavily_extract, local_manual, etc.)

Example:
1. Wikipedia contributors. "Honda H engine." *Wikipedia*, n.d. https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honda_H_engine. Retrieved: 2026-05-15. Credibility: High. Access: tavily_search.
