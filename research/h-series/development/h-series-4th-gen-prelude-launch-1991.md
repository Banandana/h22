## Summary

The fourth-generation Honda Prelude (BB1 chassis) launched on September 19, 1991 in Japan with two new H-series engines — the H22A DOHC VTEC (2.2L) and H23A SOHC (2.3L) — marking a significant displacement increase from the previous generation's 2.1L B-series. The engine lineup included the H22A Si VTEC at 200 PS, the H22A Type S at 220 PS, the H23A Si at 160 PS, and the base F22A1 at 135 PS. The car featured electronic four-wheel steering, fixed headlights (end of pop-up era), a 58/42 front/rear weight distribution, and a curvaceous design inspired by the Honda Ascot Innova. US launch followed in 1993 as the H22A1 VTEC at $15,205–$18,750. European launch was early 1992. The 4th gen sold 98,627 units in North America (1992–1996), down from 160,909 in the 3rd gen, reflecting market competition from the Accord Coupe and Integra. Despite declining sales, the 4th gen Prelude earned seven consecutive years on Car and Driver's Ten Best list (1992–1998) and was described as "the most technologically advanced, well-equipped, and highest performance Honda then available short of the Acura NSX."

## Engine Introduction: H22 and H23

### Displacement Increase Context

The 4th gen Prelude increased engine displacement from 2.1L (B21A in 3rd gen) to 2.2L (H22A) and 2.3L (H23A), moving to the new H-series architecture derived from the F-series. This represented Honda's commitment to higher power output while maintaining reliability.

## Market Reception

### Critical Acclaim

**Car and Driver Ten Best:** The Prelude appeared on Car and Driver's annual Ten Best list seven consecutive times from 1992 to 1998, demonstrating sustained critical appreciation. The biggest recurring complaint was the lack of a V-6 engine option.

**Performance Testing:** The 4th gen Prelude maintained the 3rd gen's slalom dominance tradition. While specific 4th gen slalom numbers are not publicly documented, the car was described as "significantly more powerful throughout its rpm range" even without VTEC.

**Design Reception:** The curvaceous fourth-generation design was "the most dramatic departure from the angular origami designs of prior iterations." The front fascia resembled contemporary US pony cars (particularly the Pontiac Firebird), while the tail evoked a Jaguar XJS. The interior dashboard was "allegedly inspired by Honda designers looking at the Tokyo skyline" — a dark full-width panoramic look with colored lights.

**Technology Leadership:** The 4th gen was widely praised for its technology suite:
- Electronic 4WS (replacing mechanical)
- First US DOHC VTEC engine (H22A1 in 1993)
- Upgraded brake system on VTEC models (11.1" front rotors vs 10.3")
- 8-speaker audio system with subwoofer (VTEC/SE models)
- Digital climate control (JDM)
- Leather interior (VTEC/SE models)

### Factors Contributing to Declining Sales

1. **Accord Coupe Competition:** The 6th gen Accord Coupe received exclusive body panels and shared brochures with the Prelude. Its sedan roots gave it more utility than the cramped Prelude, and a V6 option made it more appealing.
2. **Civic Si Coupe:** Considerably less expensive with better fuel economy.
3. **S2000 Competition:** More expensive but offered RWD, 6-speed, 40 extra HP, and a convertible top.
4. **Japanese Tax Regulations:** Exterior dimensions no longer complied with Japanese government "compact" vehicle regulations, adding tax burden.
5. **Rising Prices:** Premium positioning pushed prices toward luxury territory without luxury brand cachet.

### Formula One Safety Car Appearance

The 4th gen Prelude served as the safety car during the 1994 Japanese Grand Prix at Suzuka, a notable prestige moment for the model. It was also used once as a pace car at Suzuka International Racing Course in 1992.

### Key Engineering Changes from 3rd to 4th Gen

1. **Engine family swap:** B-series (B20A/B21A) → H-series (H22A/H23A/F22A1)
2. **Displacement increase:** 2.1L max → 2.3L max
3. **VTEC debut in US:** H22A1 was first DOHC VTEC offered in America (1993)
4. **4WS transition:** Mechanical → Electronic (more reliable, lighter)
5. **Headlight change:** Pop-up → Fixed (aerodynamic improvement, end of an era)
6. **Brake upgrade:** VTEC models received larger front rotors (11.1" vs 10.3") and calipers from the Honda Vigor
7. **Weight distribution:** Optimized to 58/42 front/rear

## Regional Market Differences at Launch

### Japan (September 1991)

- Full model range: S, Si, Si VTEC, Si VTEC 4WS, Si VTEC-S Limited, Si VTEC Sport Package, Si VTEC Type S
- Multiple engine options including H22A, H23A, F22A1, F22B
- Honda Verno dealership channel (performance-oriented sales network)
- 4WS adoption expected to be high (80% in 3rd gen)
- Additional road tax due to exceeding compact vehicle size regulations

### Europe (Early 1992)

- Reduced model range: 2.0i, 2.2i VTEC, 2.3i
- H22A2 (EDM) at 182 hp detuned for European emissions
- 4WS available on select trims
- Color-matched bumpers and revised lighting

### United States (1993 Model Year)

- Three trims: S (F22A1), Si (H23A1), VTEC (H22A1)
- H22A1 marked first DOHC VTEC in US market — major marketing point
- 4WS available on VTEC trim
- Leather interior standard on VTEC
- 7-speaker audio (VTEC) / 6-speaker (S/Si)
- Alloy wheels standard on Si/VTEC

### Australia

- Si (H23A1) and Si VTEC (H22A1) variants
- Si SRS with ALB and driver's airbag
- 4WS available
- Later VTi-R variant (February 1994) with H22A1

## Conflicts & Uncertainties

1. **CarGurus "1991" pricing label:** The 4th gen launched as a 1992 model year in the US. CarGurus labels these as "1991" prices, which may reflect late-1991 ordering or a cataloging inconsistency. The actual US dealer arrival was 1992.

2. **H22A Type S power:** Some sources cite 220 PS (162 kW; 217 hp) @ 7,200 rpm while others round to 217 hp. The JIS PS figure is 220 PS; SAE net horsepower is approximately 217 hp. Both refer to the same engine.

3. **H23A displacement:** Wikipedia H engine lists H23A as 2,259 cc (87mm × 95mm), but some sources reference 2.3L generically. The precise figure is 2,259 cc.

4. **Australian VTi-R launch timing:** The Australian VTi-R with H22A1 appears to have launched in February 1994, not at the initial 1991 JDM launch. This is a mid-generation addition.
