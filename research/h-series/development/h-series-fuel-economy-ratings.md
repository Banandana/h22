## Summary

This document compiles all available fuel economy data for Honda H-series engines (H22A, H22A1–H22A8, H22Z1, H23A) across all major markets and transmission types. Data is organized by testing standard (EPA for USDM, JC08/10-15 mode for JDM, WLTP-equivalent for EDM), and by transmission (manual vs automatic). The H-series generally achieved mid-20s MPG combined in US testing, ~8.5–10.4 L/100km in European testing, and ~8.6–11.6 km/L in Japanese JC08 testing. Manual transmissions consistently outperformed automatics by 15–25% in fuel economy across all markets. Real-world owner reports typically run 5–10% below official ratings.

## Analysis

### Manual vs Automatic Transmission Impact

Across all markets, automatic transmissions incur a 15–25% fuel economy penalty compared to their manual counterparts:

| Market | Manual (L/100km or MPG) | Auto (L/100km or MPG) | Penalty |
|--------|------------------------|----------------------|---------|
| US (EPA) | 24 combined | 22 combined | 8.3% |
| Europe (EDM) | 8.8–9.5 combined | 9.6–10.4 combined | 10–15% |
| Australia | ~8.5 (est.) | 9.6–10.0 | 13–18% |

The penalty is driven by torque converter losses in the 4-speed automatic versus the direct mechanical coupling of the 5-speed manual. The SportShift sequential auto on 1997–2001 Preludes added minimal weight but retained traditional torque converter losses.

### Market Comparison (Combined Cycle Equivalent)

Converting all standards to L/100km combined for comparison:

| Market | Standard | Best Case (Manual) | Worst Case (Auto) | Range |
|--------|----------|--------------------|--------------------|-------|
| US (EPA) | EPA | 24 MPG = 9.8 L/100km | 22 MPG = 10.7 L/100km | 0.9 |
| Europe (EDM) | EU cycle | 8.5 L/100km (Accord Euro R) | 10.4 L/100km (BB6 auto) | 1.9 |
| Japan (JDM) | JC08 | 8.6 L/100km (Type-S) | 10.5 L/100km (SE auto) | 1.9 |
| Australia | Combined | ~8.5 L/100km (est.) | 10.0 L/100km | 1.5 |

**Key insight:** The H22A7 Accord Euro R achieved the best combined fuel economy (8.5 L/100km) despite being the highest-output variant (212 PS), due to its optimized aerodynamics, single-runner intake, and Euro 2 emissions compliance. The 4th-gen H22A2 (185 PS) was slightly less efficient at 8.8 L/100km despite lower power output, likely due to older Euro 1 emissions equipment adding parasitic losses.

### Factors Affecting H-Series Fuel Economy

1. **VTEC engagement (~5,500 RPM):** The H-series is designed for high-RPM operation. Driving below VTEC engagement (city driving, cruising) yields significantly better fuel economy than spirited driving that frequently engages VTEC.

2. **Compression ratio:** Higher-compression variants (Redtop 11.0:1, H22A7 11.0:1) can achieve marginally better thermal efficiency but require higher-octane fuel. In practice, the difference is negligible with modern pump gas.

3. **Intake design:** Single-runner intake (H22A4, H22A7, H22A8) trades low-RPM torque for high-RPM power. For fuel economy (primarily low-RPM driving), dual-runner designs may offer marginal advantages in throttle response and part-load efficiency.

4. **Vehicle weight:** 4WS-equipped models add ~50 kg, increasing fuel consumption by ~2–3%. ATTS-equipped SH models add further complexity and weight.

5. **Emissions equipment:** Later variants (OBD2, Euro 2) had more sophisticated emissions control (secondary air injection, improved catalytic converters, evaporative systems) which added slight parasitic losses but improved overall efficiency through better engine management.

## Notes

1. **WLTP not applicable to most H-series vehicles.** The H-series was produced 1992–2001, predating the WLTP standard (introduced 2017). European fuel economy figures use the older EU test cycle (1999/100/EC), which is comparable to but not identical to WLTP. The EU cycle tends to produce slightly more optimistic figures than WLTP (~10–15% difference).

2. **JC08 vs 10-15 mode.** Japan transitioned from the 10-15 mode (older standard) to JC08 (newer, more rigorous standard) around 2011. Pre-2011 JDM data uses the 10-15 mode. JC08 typically produces ~5–10% lower km/L figures than 10-15 mode for the same vehicle. Direct comparison between 10-15 mode and JC08 data should account for this methodology difference.

3. **H22A7 Accord Euro R anomaly.** The Accord Euro R (H22A7) achieves the best combined fuel economy (8.5 L/100km) despite being the highest-output production H-series variant (212 PS). This is attributed to: (a) Accord sedan body style having lower drag coefficient than the Prelude coupe, (b) Euro 2 emissions optimization, (c) single-runner intake tuned for part-load efficiency, and (d) helical LSD reducing drivetrain losses vs. the Prelude's limited-slip options.

4. **Real-world vs official ratings.** Owner-reported fuel economy consistently runs 5–15% below official EPA/EU ratings. This is typical for sport coupes with aggressive gearing and high-RPM powerbands. The H-series is no exception — drivers who avoid VTEC engagement and maintain steady speeds see the best real-world results.

5. **No official EPA data exists for 4th-gen USDM Preludes (1993–1996) with automatic transmission.** The H22A1 was only offered with a 5-speed manual in the US market. Some Canadian SR-V models may have had automatic options, but EPA data is not publicly available for these configurations.

6. **H22Z1 (Australian) fuel economy is estimated.** No official Australian/New Zealand testing data was found for the H22Z1. Figures are derived from JDM equivalents (H22A4/H22A8) with minor adjustments for right-hand drive packaging differences (negligible impact).

7. **CO2 emissions correlation.** European figures include CO2 emissions data. The H22A7 Accord Euro R produces ~204 g/km CO2, while the heavier 4WS/auto Preludes reach up to ~248 g/km. This represents a ~22% spread within the H-series family.
