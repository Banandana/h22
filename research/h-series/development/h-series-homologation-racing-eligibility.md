## Summary

The Honda H-series engine family is eligible for a wide range of amateur, club, and historic racing series worldwide. The primary entry point for North American club racers is NASA's Honda Challenge Series (H2 class), which explicitly lists H22 engines with a minimum weight of 2,450 lbs and restricts throttle body size to 70mm. SCCA TT/Solo classes accept H22-powered vehicles under various production-based categories. FIA Historic documents recognize the H22A engine for historic competition use. SVRA Group 12 and HSR GTU classes also accommodate H22-equipped vehicles. Factory-backed racing applications include European Formula 3 (destroked to 2.0L, developed by Neil Brown Engineering), British Touring Car Championship (BTCC, 1995–1997), and Japan Touring Car Championship (JTCC, 1996–1997 championships won). Special edition approvals include JDM SiR.S spec, Type S, USDM Type SH, EDM VTi-S, and Accord Type R.

## Amateur & Club Racing Classes

### NASA Honda Challenge Series

NASA's Honda Challenge is the most accessible racing series for H22 owners. The series has three classes:

| Class | Preparation Level | Description | H22 Relevance |
|-------|------------------|-------------|---------------|
| H1 | Limited Preparation | Stock-block, stock internals, minimal mods | H22 eligible; strictest rules; best for beginners |
| H2 | Factory Preparation | OEM blocks allowed, limited aftermarket parts | **Primary H22 class**; allows cams, headers, exhaust, intake |
| H4 | Restricted Preparation | Broader modification allowance | H22 eligible; more permissive than H2 |

### SCCA Solo (Time Trial)

SCCA Solo has no mechanical restrictions — any H22-powered vehicle can compete. This makes it the most popular format for H22 track day enthusiasts. Key considerations:

| Factor | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Power restrictions | None — Solo is about driver skill, not raw power |
| Tire classes | Vary by weight/power ratio; H22 Preludes typically in Street Touring or Street Modified |
| Safety requirements | Helmet required; roll bar/cage required above certain power levels |
| Cost to enter | ~$50–100 per event + safety gear (~$500–2,000 initial investment) |

## Historic Racing Categories

### FIA Historic Technical Passport (HTP)

The FIA Historic Database lists the H22A engine under homologation code C2 (2,156 cc). This enables H22-equipped vehicles to compete in FIA-sanctioned historic events.

| FIA Document | Purpose | H22 Relevance |
|--------------|---------|---------------|
| Form 5837 | Historic Vehicle Passport application | Required for FIA HTP |
| Form 5357 | Technical data sheet | Engine specifications, chassis data |
| Form 5351 | Competition history | Race results, previous ownership |
| Form 5291 | Vehicle identification | VIN, chassis number verification |
| Form 5153 | Historical documentation | Original registration, factory specs |
| Form 307 | ASN certification | National sporting authority verification |
| Form 308 | HTP issuance | Final approval document |

#### FIA Appendix K Reference

Per FIA Appendix K Art. 4.1.4: "For any homologated car, the HTP must be accompanied by an ASN certified copy (printed on FIA stamped/watermarked paper)."

### VDCA (Vintage Drivers Club of America)

VDCA requires:
- All body parts made of same material/design as manufacturer standard
- Wheel diameter/width meeting SCCA Production Car Specs or FIA homologation
- Driver head contact areas padded
- Car registration form submitted to technical director

### CARB Executive Order A-23-154

This Executive Order certifies the H22A engine as a legal replacement powerplant for emissions testing purposes in California. It covers:
- Catalytic converter compatibility
- On-board diagnostics (OBD2) compliance
- Evaporative emissions system integration
- Labeling and documentation requirements

## Notes

- **NASA Honda Challenge H2 class** is the most relevant racing category for H22 owners in North America. The 2026 rules explicitly list H22 engines with a 2,450 lb minimum weight and 70mm max throttle body restriction.
- **FIA Historic recognition**: The H22A engine appears in the FIA Historic Database under code C2 (2,156 cc displacement). This enables H22-equipped vehicles to compete in FIA-sanctioned historic events worldwide.
- **Factory racing heritage**: The H22A's JTCC championship wins (1996–1997) and BTCC participation (1995–1997) provide proven racing pedigree. The European F3 conversion (destroked to 2.0L) directly led to the F20C (S2000) development.
- **CARB compliance**: California's CARB Executive Order A-23-154 is the key document for H22 swap legality. Without it, an H22 swap is illegal for street use in California regardless of the engine's road-legal status in its original application.
- **HSR GTU class**: The IMSA-1 (GTU) class at Historic Sportscar Racing accepts H22-powered vehicles, providing a pathway for vintage competition.
- **No FIA Group N homologation**: The Prelude was never FIA Group N homologated as a rally car. The H22A's racing credentials come from touring car (JTCC/BTCC) and formula (F3) applications, not rallying.
- **Modern Prelude special editions**: Honda released limited edition Preludes in 2024+ (ON Limited Edition, Limited Edition) — these are modern reinterpretations, not H-series vehicles, but carry the model name heritage.
