## Summary

The Honda H-series engine family was manufactured primarily at Honda's Tochigi Plant in Tochigi Prefecture, Japan, which served as the central production hub for all H-series variants from 1991 through 2001. The Tochigi Plant was Honda's fourth domestic factory (established December 1970), specializing in powertrain components and exclusive production of the NSX in 1990. In 1992, it consolidated three predecessor facilities (Mooka, Takanezawa, and Haga plants). Outside Japan, Honda's Swindon UK Manufacturing (HUM) plant — established 1985, began engine production July 1989 — possessed alloy engine block and head casting capability and assembled the European Accord Type R CH1 (H22A7). Whether the H22A7 engine itself was cast/machined at Swindon or imported from Tochigi remains unconfirmed. Honda's Anna Engine Plant in Ohio produced a 2.2-liter Accord 4-cylinder engine at its one-millionth milestone in February 1994, but this was likely an F-series F22B unit rather than an H-series engine. No confirmed H-series engine production occurred at Suzuka Factory, Thailand, or any other international facility during the 1991–2001 production run.

### H-Series Production at Tochigi

The Tochigi Plant was the primary and likely sole manufacturing location for all H-series engines during their 1991–2001 production run. This includes:

- **H22A (Redtop/Blacktop)** — JDM Prelude SiR, Type S, Accord SiR
- **H22A1** — USDM/CAN/AUS Prelude VTEC, SR-V, VTi-R
- **H22A2** — EDM Prelude 2.2i VTEC
- **H22A3** — Rare 1996 KU-code variants
- **H22A4** — USDM 5th gen Prelude Base, Type-SH, SE
- **H22A5** — EDM Prelude 2.2VTi/VTi-S
- **H22A7** — EDM Accord Type R CH1 (engine origin may differ — see below)
- **H22A8** — EDM Prelude 2.2VTi/VTi-S (1999–2001)
- **H22Z1** — Australian VTi-R
- **H23A DOHC VTEC** — JDM Accord Wagon SiR CH9 (blue top)
- **H23A1** — USDM/CAN/AUS Prelude Si

### Engine Manufacturing Capability

The Swindon plant had significant engine manufacturing infrastructure:

- **Alloy engine block casting** — Two high-pressure 2,000-tonne presses working in tandem; operated 24/7 with always-molten metal
- **Low-pressure head casting processes** — Metal only partly molten, with heat treatment after casting
- **Engine machining** — Blocks and heads were cast and machined on-site
- **Engine assembly** — Complete engine assembly lines
- **Recycling** — Casting sand was sold to local council for road repairs

By 2008, the plant had produced 2 million engines. British parts including engine blocks and heads were exported to Honda's Turkish plant for local Civic assembly, and complete engines went to Mexico for HR-V SUVs.

### H22A7 in European Accord Type R — Unclear Engine Origin

The European Accord Type R CH1 (1998–2002) used the H22A7 engine (212 PS, red valve cover). The vehicle was assembled at Swindon. However, the engine's origin is uncertain:

- **Possibility A:** Engine imported from Tochigi Plant, Japan. Honda often shipped specialized performance engines from Japan even when vehicle assembly occurred overseas.
- **Possibility B:** Engine cast/machined at Swindon. The plant had full engine manufacturing capability by 1992 and was producing engines for the European Accord since October 1992.

**Conflict note:** The existing h-series-european-market-introduction.md (T-044) states the Accord Type R was "built at Swindon UK (HUKM plant)" without clarifying whether this refers to vehicle assembly only or engine manufacturing as well. The Swindon plant did cast its own alloy blocks and heads starting in 1989, making engine production plausible. However, no definitive source confirms whether the H22A7 specifically was cast at Swindon or imported from Japan.

**Assessment:** Given that the H22A7 was a high-performance variant (212 PS, 11.0:1 compression) and that Honda typically centralized performance engine production at Tochigi during this period, the most likely scenario is that the H22A7 engine was manufactured at Tochigi and shipped to Swindon for vehicle assembly. This is consistent with Honda's practice of keeping specialized performance engines in Japan while assembling vehicles locally for export markets. Further archival research would be needed to confirm.

### The 2.2-Liter Accord Milestone

Honda's Anna Engine Plant produced its one-millionth engine on February 14, 1994 — a 2.2-liter Accord 4-cylinder. This timing coincides with the early production years of the H22A1 (USDM Prelude VTEC, introduced 1993).

**Ambiguity:** The 2.2-liter Accord 4-cylinder produced at Anna in the early 1990s was most likely the **F22B** engine (used in North American Accord sedans/coupes), not the H22A1. The F22B shared the same 87mm bore and similar displacement (2,158 cc vs 2,157 cc) but was a different engine family derived from the F-series architecture. The H22A1 was used in the Prelude, not the Accord.

**Conclusion:** While the Anna Engine Plant produced 2.2-liter engines during the H-series era, there is no confirmed evidence that any H-series engine was manufactured at Anna. The 2.2L Accord engine was almost certainly the F22B. All H-series engines for the USDM Prelude (H22A1, H22A4) were likely imported from Tochigi, Japan.

## Other Potential Facilities — No Confirmed H-Series Production

### Suzuka Factory, Mie Prefecture, Japan

- Honda's second factory (April 1960)
- Produced Integra (1985), Civic, City, Today, Super Cub
- Currently produces N-Box, Fit, Shuttle
- **No confirmed H-series engine production** during 1991–2001
- In 2003–2004, engine production for niche cars (NSX, Insight, S2000) was transferred from Honda Engineering to Suzuka, but this was after H-series production ended

**Source:** Honda Global — Suzuka Factory: https://global.honda/en/about/group/suzuka.html, retrieved 2026-05-15

### Thailand (Prachinburi)

- Honda's Thai facility produces cars and petrol engines for Southeast Asia and Australia distribution
- **No confirmed H-series engine production** — H-series was discontinued globally by 2001, and Thai production focused on more economical engine families

### Other International Facilities

- **China (Guangzhou/Wuhan):** Joint ventures producing CRV, Civic, Spirior engines (2.0L, 2.4L) — post-H-series era
- **Turkey (Gebze):** Car assembly only, no engine production
- **Malaysia (Malacca):** Car assembly only
- **Brazil:** Closed car production facilities (2021)
- **India:** Motorcycles and scooters only

## Notes

- **Primary production hub:** The Tochigi Plant in Tochigi Prefecture was unequivocally the primary and likely sole manufacturing location for H-series engines throughout their 1991–2001 production run.
- **Swindon uncertainty:** The H22A7 engine origin for the European Accord Type R CH1 is the most significant gap. The Swindon plant had full engine casting and assembly capability, but no definitive source confirms whether the H22A7 was cast there or imported from Tochigi.
- **Anna Engine Plant clarification:** The 2.2-liter Accord 4-cylinder milestone at Anna (Feb 1994) was almost certainly the F22B engine, not an H-series unit. The F22B powered North American Accord sedans/coupes, while the H22A1 powered the USDM Prelude.
- **No overseas H-series engine production confirmed:** Despite Honda's extensive global manufacturing network, no H-series engine production outside Japan has been confirmed during the 1991–2001 period.
- **Recommended follow-up:** Archival research at Honda's technical library or consultation with Honda engineering archives could resolve the H22A7/Swindon question definitively.
