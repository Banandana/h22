## Summary

This document covers the complete ecosystem of Honda technician training programs, certification levels, and educational infrastructure relevant to servicing H-series engines (H22A, H22A1–H22A8, H22Z1, H23A). The training landscape spans multiple tiers: Honda's proprietary dealer programs (PACT, IST, STC), Japan's formal technical colleges (Honda Technical College Kanto and Kansai, founded 1976), industry-standard certifications (ASE), and the broader automotive education framework (NATEF accreditation, AYES). At the dealership level, technicians progressed through a structured certification ladder — Certified Technician → Master Technician — with ongoing recency requirements. The Individualized Skills Training (IST) system provided modular, self-paced instruction on specific Honda systems including VTEC technology, FRM liner service procedures, OBD1/OBD2 diagnostics, and ECU programming. In Japan, Honda Technical College graduates received direct placement into Honda Verno dealerships (the higher-end sales channel that carried Prelude and Accord SiR models). In North America, the PACT program at community colleges delivered ~85% of manufacturer training before students entered paid dealership internships.

## Honda's Dealer Technician Training Infrastructure

### Professional Automotive Career Training (PACT)

The Honda PACT (Professional Automotive Career Training) program was American Honda's flagship technician development initiative. It was a cooperative partnership among American Honda Motor Co., Inc., local community/technical colleges, and Honda/Acura dealerships.

**Program Structure:**

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Duration** | 2 years (accelerated, full-time) |
| **Format** | Classroom theory + hands-on lab + paid dealership internship |
| **Delivery** | Community college curriculum co-developed with American Honda |
| **Graduate Outcome** | ~85% of manufacturer training required for Honda/Acura certification; prepared for ASE exams |
| **Certifications Earned** | Up to 10 Honda/Acura certifications + 2+ ASE certifications |
| **GPA Requirement** | 2.0 minimum for dealership internship placement |

**Curriculum Components:**

| Course Area | Topics Covered |
|-------------|---------------|
| Shop Fundamentals & Minor Service | Dealership organizational structure, service manuals, electronic troubleshooting manuals, service bulletins |
| Light Line Technician | Express service operations, multi-point inspections, factory scheduled maintenance |
| Engine Repair | Upper/lower end rebuild, machining operations, valve train assembly, FRM liner considerations |
| Engine Performance Analysis | Circuit performance prediction from schematics, diagnostic flow charts, Honda Interactive Network Training modules |
| Automatic Transmission Systems | Torque converter, planetary gearsets, hydraulic control, electronically controlled shifts |
| Manual Drivetrain & Axles | Clutch, transmission, differential, driveshaft, CV joints |
| Electrical Systems / Diagnosis | Circuit analysis, wiring diagrams, testing procedures, Honda-specific electrical architecture |
| Brake Systems | Hydraulic service, ABS, brake pad/rotor replacement, caliper service |
| Suspension & Steering | Strut assemblies, control arms, ball joints, alignment, 4WS systems |
| Air Conditioning Systems | Refrigerant handling, compressor service, heater core, climate control |
| Advanced Electrical & Electronics | Hybrid/Green Technologies, Telematics, advanced diagnostics |
| Transmissions & Drivelines | Final drive, limited-slip differentials, ATTS (Active Torque Transfer System) |

**Internship Component:** Students spent approximately 50% of program time working at sponsoring Honda/Acura dealerships. They were paid hourly wages with incentive pay raises based on ability and performance. Dealers appointed a contact person to guide students and maintain communication with the college.

**Key Differentiator:** American Honda donated new vehicles directly to PACT colleges, ensuring students trained on the latest vehicle technology — critical for H-series models introduced in 1992 (4th gen Prelude) and 1997 (5th gen Prelude).

### Individualized Skills Training (IST)

The Individualized Skills Training (IST) system was Honda's proprietary modular training platform used across all PACT colleges and active dealership technician development. IST was designed as a self-paced learning system where students/technicians worked through individual skill modules at their own speed, with instructor assistance and evaluation available as needed.

**IST Module Categories (representative):**

| Category | Example Modules | Relevance to H-Series |
|----------|----------------|----------------------|
| **Engine Operation & Dynamics** | Fundamental engine principles, DOHC/VTEC theory | Core knowledge for H-series DOHC VTEC engines |
| **VTEC Technology** | VTEC solenoid operation, oil pressure switching, cam profile differences | Critical for H22A/H22A1/H22A4 service and diagnostics |
| **FRM Liner Service** | Cylinder liner inspection, wear measurement, sleeve installation procedures | Essential for H-series block rebuilds (FRM is unique to Honda's aluminum blocks) |
| **OBD1 Diagnostics** | PGM-FI systems, socketed ECU chips, code retrieval | For 1992–1996 H22A1 vehicles |
| **OBD2 Diagnostics** | Scan tool operation, live data streams, readiness monitors | For 1997–2001 H22A4 vehicles |
| **ECU Programming** | Flash memory chip reprogramming, calibration maps | For P5M/P51/P13 ECU variants |
| **Timing Belt Service** | Belt replacement, tensioner procedures, TDC alignment | Critical interference-engine maintenance |
| **Valve Adjustment** | Solid-lifter clearance measurement, adjustment procedure | Periodic maintenance requirement |
| **4WS Systems** | Four-wheel steering hydraulics/electronics | Integrated with H-series in 4WS Preludes |
| **ATTS** | Active Torque Transfer System | SH model exclusive, H22A4-powered |

**Assessment Method:** Each IST module concluded with a practical performance evaluation. Successful completion was recorded on the student's Dealer Personal Tracking System (DPTS) record.

### iN Online University / Dealer Personal Tracking System (DPTS)

Honda's online training platform, iN Online University, served as the centralized tracking system for all dealer technician training progress. The Dealer Personal Tracking System (DPTS) maintained individual records of completed IST modules, earned certifications, and training recency. This system was referenced by PACT colleges to verify student progress and by dealerships to assign appropriate work experience.

**Key Features:**
- Real-time tracking of training module completion
- Certification status management
- Recency verification for Master Technician plaque validity
- Integration with ServiceExpress training materials

### Honda Service Technical College (STC)

Honda Service Technical College (STC) was an additional training avenue offered by American Honda beyond the PACT college partnership. STC provided on-site or off-site technical training specifically for existing dealership technicians who wanted to advance beyond entry-level certification. STC courses covered advanced diagnostic procedures, new model introductions, and technology-specific deep dives (e.g., VTEC engineering, hybrid systems).

## Honda Certification Levels

### Certified Technician

The entry-level dealership certification. Technicians achieved this status by completing foundational IST modules covering basic maintenance, minor service, and standard operating procedures. Certified Technicians could perform:

- Oil changes and fluid top-offs
- Brake pad/rotor replacement
- Basic suspension component replacement
- Battery service
- Tire rotation and basic alignment checks
- Multi-point inspections

### Master Technician

The highest level of dealership technician certification. Achieving Master Technician status required completing approximately 100% of available Honda/Acura IST modules across all system categories. Master Technicians were expected to handle:

- Complex electrical diagnostics
- Engine performance analysis with scan tools
- VTEC system troubleshooting
- OBD1/OBD2 deep diagnostics
- ECU programming and flashing
- Advanced transmission diagnostics
- Hybrid system service (later models)
- FRM liner-related service procedures

**Recency Requirement:** Beginning April 1, 2018, Master Technician certification became valid for a 5-year period requiring continued training. Prior to this, Master Technician status was lifetime. This change reflected the increasing complexity of modern Honda vehicles.

**Plaque Recognition:** Master Technicians received a physical plaque from Honda. The post-2018 plaques feature both "Achieved" and "Valid Through" dates.

**Sources:**
- Honda Professionals — Master Technician Certification Changes: https://hondaprofessionals.com/email/masterTechCert/page-announcement-newMTCertChanges_Honda.html, retrieved 2026-05-15

The National Institute for Automotive Service Excellence (ASE) provides independent, industry-wide certification that Honda recognizes and incorporates into its training framework.

**ASE Certification Structure:**

| Test Code | Subject Area | Relevance to H-Series |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| A1 | Engine Repair | Direct — H-series engine rebuild, valve adjustment, timing belt |
| A2 | Automatic Transmission | Direct — H-series automatic transmission service |
| A3 | Manual Drivetrain | Direct — H-series manual transmission, clutch, differential |
| A4 | Suspension & Steering | Direct — H-series suspension, 4WS systems |
| A5 | Brakes | Direct — H-series brake service |
| A6 | Electrical/Electronic Systems | Direct — H-series electrical diagnostics, ECU |
| A7 | Heating & Air Conditioning | Indirect — HVAC service applicable to all Honda vehicles |
| A8 | Engine Performance | Direct — H-series engine performance diagnosis, OBD1/OBD2 |
| G1 | Maintenance & Light Repair | Express Service certification foundation |
| L1-L4 | Large Diesel (not applicable) | N/A for H-series |
| C1 | Collision Repair (not applicable) | N/A for H-series |
| P1-P2 | Parts Specialist | Service department support roles |

**ASE History Relevant to H-Era:**

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 1972 | ASE founded; first tests offered May/November |
| 1983 | NATEF (National Automotive Technicians Education Foundation) founded; began accrediting training programs |
| 1990 | First engine machinist test (M1 — cylinder head specialist) introduced |

| 1997 | ASE and NATEF began accrediting in-service automotive training via CASE program |

**Honda-ASE Integration:** Honda PACT graduates are prepared for ASE Master certification (A1–A8). Honda later established formal credit equivalencies — ASE G1, L1, L2, L3, L4, C1, P2 certifications count as course credit toward specific Honda training modules, reducing duplication.

## Honda Technical College (Japan)

### Overview

Honda Technical College (本田技術学院, *Honda Gijutsu Gakuin*) was established in 1976 by Honda founder Soichiro Honda, guided by the philosophy that education should nurture individuals welcomed by society. The school was founded with the explicit mission of developing engineers and service technicians who are trusted and valued by people — not merely trained to perform maintenance tasks.

**Two Campuses:**

| Campus | Location | Established | Focus |
|--------|----------|-------------|-------|
| **Kanto** | Fujimino, Saitama Prefecture (5 Chome-2-2 Tsurugaoka) | 1976 | First-class auto mechanic training, R&D, service engineering |
| **Kansai** | Osaka Prefecture | Later expansion | Automobile maintenance, international student programs |

**Faculty:** 62 instructors (as of 2021)

**Graduate Outcomes:** Graduates receive qualifications including:
- First-class small car mechanic (国家一級自動車整備士)
- Second-class auto mechanic (gasoline/diesel)
- Second-class motorcycle mechanic
- Honda Automobile Service Engineer Level 2
- Honda Automobile Service Engineer Level 3
- Honda Motorcycle Engineer Level 3

**Employment:** Honda Technical College graduates enjoy exceptionally high placement rates at Honda Motor Co., Ltd. — comparable to national universities and prestigious private universities. Graduates enter Honda's manufacturing divisions, service departments (quality analysis, complex repair), and Honda Research Institute (vehicle development).

**Historic Challenge:** In its 50th anniversary year (2026), students participated in the Rallye Monte-Carlo Historique — a world-renowned historic rally event — demonstrating the college's emphasis on real-world, hands-on experience beyond classroom learning.

### Connection to H-Series

Honda Technical College graduates entering Honda's service division would have been trained on the H-series engines during their tenure (1992–2001). The college's curriculum was updated to reflect new engine introductions, and H-series-specific training would have covered:

- DOHC VTEC valvetrain adjustment procedures
- FRM cylinder liner service limitations
- VTEC solenoid and oil pressure switch diagnostics
- OBD1 and OBD2 diagnostic system transitions

## H-Series-Specific Service Knowledge Areas

### VTEC System Service

VTEC (Variable Valve Timing and Electronic Control) was the signature technology of the H-series and required dedicated training:

| Service Area | Key Knowledge |
|-------------|--------------|
| **VTEC Solenoid** | Electro-hydraulic solenoid operation, oil pressure switch integration, gasket replacement at every timing belt service, solenoid screen cleaning |
| **VTEC Oil Pressure** | Minimum 30 psi for engagement, oil pressure sensor function, wiring diagnostics |
| **VTEC Engagement Parameters** | RPM threshold (~5,500 RPM), vehicle speed interlock (>5 km/h), engine temperature interlock (>40°C), throttle position requirement (>20%) |
| **Camshaft Profiles** | Difference between low-lift (torque) and high-lift (power) profiles, VTEC piston engagement mechanism |
| **Troubleshooting** | P1259 code diagnosis, oil pressure verification, solenoid resistance testing (14–30 ohms), wiring continuity checks |

### FRM Liner Service

The Fiber-Reinforced Metal (FRM) cylinder liner technology was unique to Honda's aluminum block engines and required specialized knowledge:

| Service Area | Key Knowledge |
|-------------|--------------|
| **FRM Characteristics** | Lightweight, good heat transfer, but difficult to machine with standard honing equipment |
| **Wear Symptoms** | Oil consumption, loss of compression |
| **Service Limitation** | Standard honing cannot machine FRM liners; rebuilds require aftermarket sleeve solutions (RS Machine sleeves) |
| **Preventive Care** | Avoid overheating, proper break-in procedures, regular oil changes |

### Timing Belt Service

Critical maintenance for the H-series interference engine:

| Service Area | Key Knowledge |
|-------------|--------------|
| **Interval** | 60,000–90,000 miles (30,000 for performance use) |
| **Tensioner Types** | 1992–1996: hydraulic auto-tensioner (failure-prone); 1997+: improved hydraulic tensioner |
| **Upgrade Options** | H23 manual tensioner kit, KS Tuned aftermarket tensioner |
| **Consequence of Failure** | Catastrophic engine damage (interference engine) |
| **TDC Alignment** | Crankshaft holding tool, cam gear holder, 5mm punch technique |

### OBD1 vs OBD2 Diagnostic Transition

H-series technicians had to master two different diagnostic systems during the production run:

| Aspect | OBD1 (1992–1996) | OBD2 (1997–2001) |
|--------|------------------|------------------|
| **Connector** | 32-pin (P5M/P51 ECUs) | 32-pin + 2-pin connectors |
| **ECU Memory** | Socketed EPROM chips (tunable) | Soldered memory (some variants with immobilizer) |
| **Diagnostic Tool** | Basic code reader, flash programmer | HDS (Honda Diagnostic System), Genisys scan tool |
| **Capabilities** | Trouble code retrieval only | Live data streams, fuel trim monitoring, immobilizer management |
| **Training Focus** | Chip swapping, basic circuit testing | Scan tool operation, data stream interpretation |

## Training Equipment and Facilities

### PACT College Facility Requirements

American Honda supplied PACT colleges with:
- New vehicles (donated directly from manufacturers)
- Special service tools (SST)
- Access to technical data and service information

Evergreen Valley College and other PACT colleges sent students for one week of training per year at Honda's state-of-the-art factory training center in French Camp, California. Students provided their own transportation. This facility offered:
- Hands-on training on current-model Honda vehicles
- Access to factory special tools and equipment
- Instruction by Honda-trained instructors
- Exposure to the latest service information and diagnostic procedures

## Notes

- **No single comprehensive public source exists** for the complete history of Honda's dealer technician training programs. Most information comes from individual PACT college program descriptions, Honda's own training platform documentation, and industry publications. This represents a documented gap in publicly accessible H-series documentation.
- **PACT program evolution:** The PACT program described here reflects its current form (2020s). The program existed in earlier forms during the H-series production era (1992–2001), but specific curriculum details from that period are not readily available in public sources. The core structure — college classroom + dealership internship — likely remained consistent.
- **Japanese vs. North American training divergence:** Honda Technical College in Japan was a standalone vocational school with direct employment pipelines into Honda Motor Co. North American PACT colleges were community college partnerships with dealership internship components. The training philosophies differed: Japan emphasized engineering development alongside service skills; North America emphasized practical dealership readiness.
- **FRM liner service gap:** No mainstream training program (PACT, Honda Technical College, or ASE) appears to have included specific FRM liner machining instruction. This is because FRM liners cannot be machined with standard equipment — they require aftermarket sleeve solutions. Training focused on FRM identification, wear assessment, and referral to specialized machine shops.
- **Dealer parts availability impact:** Multiple independent sources confirm that H-series parts were rarely stocked at individual Honda dealerships, requiring special orders. This affected training effectiveness — technicians learned procedures but often couldn't practice them immediately due to parts delays.
- **Recommended follow-up:** Obtaining copies of Honda's original PACT curriculum documents from the 1992–2001 period (possibly through Honda's corporate archives or former PACT instructors) would provide definitive information on H-era-specific training content.
