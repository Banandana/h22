# H-Series Recall History: Manufacturer Recalls and Safety Campaigns

## Summary

Research into NHTSA recall databases, Center for Auto Safety records, and Honda-Tech forum archives reveals that **no formal manufacturer recalls specifically targeted the H-series engine block or its core mechanical components** (FRM liners, timing belt tensioners, oil consumption). However, several broad NHTSA safety recalls affected vehicles equipped with H-series engines (primarily 1997–1999 Honda Prelude), covering ignition switches, suspension ball joints, and lighting accessories. Honda addressed known reliability concerns through **service bulletins and warranty extensions** rather than formal recall campaigns.

## NHTSA Safety Recalls Affecting H-Series Vehicles

### 1. NHTSA 99V069000 — Lower Ball Joint Failure (May 10, 1999)

- **Component:** Suspension → Front → Control Arm → Lower Ball Joint
- **Affected vehicles:** 1997–1998 Honda Prelude (H22A4-equipped BB6 chassis), plus other Honda/Acura models
- **Defect:** Premature ball joint wear; in worst case, ball joint separation causing front suspension collapse
- **Safety consequence:** Loss of vehicle control, potential crash
- **Remedy:** Dealers replaced front lower suspension ball joints
- **NHTSA complaints:** Multiple reports of ball joint failure, some resulting in loss of control incidents. Several complainants noted dealers refusing repair due to VIN not matching recall scope.
- **Sources:** [Center for Auto Safety — 1997 Honda Prelude](https://www.autosafety.org/vehicle-safety-check/1997-honda-prelude/) (accessed 2026-05-15); [Center for Auto Safety — 1998 Honda Prelude](https://www.autosafety.org/vehicle-safety-check/1998-honda-prelude/) (accessed 2026-05-15)

### 2. NHTSA 02V120000 — Ignition Switch Contact Degradation (May 16, 2002)

- **Component:** Electrical System → Ignition → Switch
- **Affected vehicles:** Multiple Honda/Acura models including 1997–1999 Prelude
- **Defect:** Electrical contacts in ignition switch degraded due to high electrical current during startup
- **Safety consequence:** Engine stalling, inability to restart
- **Remedy:** Dealers replaced ignition switch
- **NHTSA complaints:** Numerous reports of stalling at highway speeds, stalling at stoplights, intermittent no-start conditions. Some complainants noted VIN excluded from recall scope despite identical symptoms.
- **Sources:** [Center for Auto Safety — 1998 Honda Prelude](https://www.autosafety.org/vehicle-safety-check/1998-honda-prelude/) (accessed 2026-05-15)

### 3. NHTSA 05V025000 — Ignition Switch Interlock Defect (January 28, 2005)

- **Component:** Electrical System → Ignition → Switch
- **Affected vehicles:** 1997–1999 Honda Prelude, Acura TL, Honda Accord (MY 1999–2001)
- **Defect:** Interlock operation may not function properly, allowing ignition key to be turned to "OFF" and removed without shifting transmission to Park
- **Safety consequence:** Vehicle rollaway if driver leaves transmission in non-Park position
- **Remedy:** Dealers inspected interlock function and installed redesigned interlock lever
- **NHTSA investigation:** DP 04-006 investigated defect petition; ODI found interlock systems made by Honda's Japanese lock facility contained defective internal components. U.S.-manufactured interlock systems were not included.
- **Population:** ~402,525 vehicles across all affected models
- **Sources:** [Center for Auto Safety — 1997 Honda Prelude](https://www.autosafety.org/vehicle-safety-check/1997-honda-prelude/) (accessed 2026-05-15); NHTSA DP 04-006 documentation

### 4. NHTSA 05V018000 — Additional Ignition-Related Recall

- Referenced in NHTSA complaints for 1997 Prelude (consumer received recall repair work on this campaign date)
- Specific details require direct NHTSA document lookup
- **Source:** [Center for Auto Safety — 1997 Honda Prelude complaint database](https://www.autosafety.org/vehicle-safety-check/1997-honda-prelude/) (accessed 2026-05-15)

## Non-Recall Manufacturer Actions

### FRM Liner Service Bulletin SB618958

- Honda issued service bulletin referencing FRM sleeve honing procedures for oil consumption issues
- Referenced on Honda-Tech forums (thread title: "Honing FRM sleeves.... Service Bulletin SB618958")
- Honda-Tech access blocked by Cloudflare; bulletin details not fully extractable
- MotorTrend Wrenchin' article confirms FRM liner oil consumption is a known issue affecting H22A/H23A engines
- **Key finding:** Honda chose dealer-level service bulletin over formal recall for FRM-related oil consumption
- **Sources:** [Honda-Tech forum thread](https://honda-tech.com/forums/honda-prelude-4/honing-frm-sleeves-service-bulletin-sb618958-18939-a-405266/) (accessed 2026-05-15, Cloudflare-blocked); [MotorTrend — Honda FRM Liners](https://www.motortrend.com/how-to/htup-0907-honda-frm-liners) (accessed 2026-05-15)

### Timing Belt Tensioner — Service Bulletin #01-010

- Honda service bulletin recognized auto-tensioner failure as a known issue
- NHTSA complaint documented consumer with service bulletin acknowledging problem
- Tensioner failure caused timing jump, valve-to-piston damage, complete engine overhaul required ($2,300 cost to consumer)
- Consumer stated owner's manual recommended 105,000-mile inspection interval, but tensioner failed at 79,000 miles
- Honda did not issue formal recall; covered under service bulletin
- **Source:** [Center for Auto Safety — 1998 Honda Prelude complaint](https://www.autosafety.org/vehicle-safety-check/1998-honda-prelude/) (accessed 2026-05-15)

### Automatic Transmission Warranty Extension

- Honda extended warranty coverage for automatic transmissions in 1999–2001 Prelude models
- 1997–1998 models excluded from warranty extension despite identical transmission design (M6HA SportShift)
- NHTSA complaints document widespread transmission failures at low mileage (40,000–80,000 miles)
- Multiple complainants noted Honda acknowledged the problem but limited remedy to later model years
- One complainant noted Honda recalled preludes in Turkey for the same transmission issue and replaced with updated MH6A units, but did not extend remedy to U.S. market
- **Sources:** [Center for Auto Safety — 1997 Honda Prelude](https://www.autosafety.org/vehicle-safety-check/1997-honda-prelude/) (accessed 2026-05-15); [Center for Auto Safety — 1998 Honda Prelude](https://www.autosafety.org/vehicle-safety-check/1998-honda-prelude/) (accessed 2026-05-15)

## Key Findings

1. **No engine-specific recalls.** No NHTSA recall campaign directly addressed H-series engine block integrity, FRM liner degradation, timing belt tensioner failure, or oil consumption. These were handled via service bulletins and warranty extensions.

2. **Three major OEM recalls affected H-series vehicles:** Ball joint suspension failure (99V069000), ignition switch contact degradation (02V120000), and ignition switch interlock defect (05V025000). All three are safety-critical but none target the engine itself.

3. **Service bulletin approach.** Honda consistently preferred dealer-level service bulletins (SB618958 for FRM, #01-010 for tensioner) over formal recalls for engine-related issues. This allowed Honda to address problems without triggering formal recall reporting requirements.

4. **Transmission issue asymmetry.** Honda extended transmission warranties for 1999–2001 Prelude models but excluded 1997–1998 models with the same M6HA SportShift transmission. The same transmission was recalled in Turkey but not the U.S. market.

5. **Complaint volume spike.** 1997–1999 Preludes generated 214 NHTSA complaints combined (58% of all Prelude complaints), concentrated in automatic transmission failures, ignition switch stalling, and suspension issues.

6. **Oil consumption not classified as safety defect.** FRM liner-related oil consumption, while widespread and well-documented in enthusiast communities, was never classified by Honda or NHTSA as a safety-related defect warranting recall.

## Conflicts and Limitations

- **Honda-Tech access blocked.** The primary Honda-Tech forum thread referencing SB618958 is behind Cloudflare protection, preventing full extraction of the service bulletin text.
- **Service bulletin details incomplete.** Without direct access to Honda's dealer communication system, exact wording and scope of service bulletins cannot be fully verified.
- **NHTSA complaint data is self-reported.** Complaint counts reflect owner reporting behavior, not actual failure rates. Older vehicles may have fewer complaints simply because owners no longer bother filing.
- **Turkish recall discrepancy.** Multiple complainants reported Honda recalled preludes in Turkey for transmission issues but did not extend the remedy to North America. This claim is difficult to independently verify without Turkish regulatory records.
