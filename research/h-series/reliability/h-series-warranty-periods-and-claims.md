# H-Series Warranty Periods and Common Warranty Claims

## Summary

Research into Honda's factory warranty structure during the H-series production era (1991–2001), industry-wide warranty claim data, and enthusiast forum reports reveals that **Honda's standard warranty coverage was consistent throughout the H-series lifecycle** — 3-year/36,000-mile bumper-to-bumper and 5-year/60,000-mile powertrain — but **the most significant warranty action specific to H-series vehicles was an automatic transmission extension covering 2000–2001 models**. Oil consumption and timing belt tensioner failures were the two most commonly reported engine-related issues, both handled through service bulletins rather than formal warranty extensions. Forum evidence suggests warranty claims for oil consumption were frequently denied unless consumption exceeded dealer-defined thresholds.

## Honda Factory Warranty Structure (1990s–2000s)

### Standard New Vehicle Limited Warranty

Honda's warranty terms during the entire H-series production period remained stable:

| Coverage Type | Duration | Mileage Limit | What It Covered |
|---|---|---|---|
| Bumper-to-Bumper (New Vehicle Limited Warranty) | 3 years | 36,000 miles | Defects in materials and workmanship for virtually all vehicle components, including the 12-volt battery |
| Powertrain Limited Warranty | 5 years | 60,000 miles | Engine block/head/internal parts, timing belt/chain, flywheel, valve covers, oil pan, oil pump, intake/exhaust manifolds, engine mounts, PCM, water pump, fuel pump, seals/gaskets; transmission/transaxle case/internal parts, torque converter, PCM, seals/gaskets; front-wheel-drive system (final drive, driveshafts, CV joints, front hubs) |
| Rust Perforation Limited Warranty | 5 years | Unlimited mileage | Body panels rusting completely through from inside out |
| Emissions Control Systems (Federal) | 2 years / 7 years | 24,000 miles / 70,000 miles | Evaporative system, catalytic converter, onboard diagnostic system; select components extended to 7 years/70,000 miles |
| Accessory Limited Warranty | 3 years | 36,000 miles | Genuine Honda accessories installed at time of purchase |
| Replacement Parts Limited Warranty | 1 year | 12,000 miles | Parts installed by a Honda dealer |
| Remanufactured Parts Limited Warranty | 3 years | 36,000 miles | Honda-approved replacement components |
| Seat Belt Limited Warranty | Useful life of vehicle | N/A | Any seat belt failing to function properly |
| Original Equipment Battery | 36 months | N/A (pro-rated) | 100% coverage first 24 months, pro-rated thereafter |
| Original Equipment Muffler | Lifetime | N/A | As long as original purchaser owns the vehicle |

**Source:** [1992 Honda Warranties booklet](http://techinfo.honda.com/rjanisis/pubs/OM/CV9292/CV9292O00184A.pdf) (accessed 2026-05-15); [2016 Honda Warranty Basebook](https://owners.honda.com/Documentum/Warranty/Handbooks/AWL00476.pdf) (accessed 2026-05-15); [2022 Honda Warranty Basebook](https://owners.honda.com/Documentum/Warranty/Handbooks/2022_Honda_Warranty_Basebook.pdf) (accessed 2026-05-15).

### Key Implications for H-Series Owners

- **Bumper-to-bumper expired quickly.** An owner purchasing a 1992 Prelude Si VTEC at $23,000 lost full coverage after 3 years/36,000 miles (approximately 1995–1996 or ~72,000 miles). Any non-powertrain defect occurring after that point was the owner's responsibility.
- **Powertrain coverage was meaningful.** The 5-year/60,000-mile powertrain warranty covered the engine block, head, internal parts, timing belt, water pump, and seals/gaskets — directly relevant to the H-series. A 1992 model would retain powertrain coverage until roughly 1997 or 60,000 miles.
- **Timing belt was explicitly covered.** The timing belt and its cover are listed under powertrain coverage. However, Honda's maintenance schedule recommended inspection at 105,000 miles — **after** the powertrain warranty had expired for most owners. This created a gap where timing belt tensioner failure (documented in NHTSA complaint and service bulletin #01-010) occurred outside warranty coverage.
- **Emissions warranties were longer.** Select emissions components carried 7-year/70,000-mile coverage, which could have been relevant for PCV valve, breather chamber, and evaporative system components.

## Automatic Transmission Warranty Extension (2002)

### The Extension

In September 2002, American Honda announced an extended warranty on select automatic transmissions affecting H-series-equipped vehicles:

**Vehicles covered:**

| Model | Years | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Honda Prelude | 2000–2001 | H22A4-equipped, M6HA SportShift automatic |
| Honda Accord | 2000–2001 | F22B/F23A engines |
| Honda Odyssey | 1999–2001 | F23A engine |
| Acura 3.2 TL | 2000–2002 | C32B engine |
| Acura 3.2 CL | 2001–2002 | C32B engine |

**Extent of coverage:** Extended transmission warranty beyond the standard 5-year/60,000-mile powertrain term. A subsequent class-action lawsuit settlement (2006) extended coverage further to **93 months or 109,000 miles** from the original in-service date.

**Prevalence:** Only approximately **2%** of these vehicles experienced transmission problems severe enough to trigger the extension.

**Affected transmission:** M6HA SportShift (4-speed automatic with manual mode / "Tiptronic" functionality). This is the same transmission referenced in the recall history (T-052) as having asymmetric warranty treatment — 1999–2001 models were included in the extension while 1997–1998 models with the identical transmission design were excluded.

**Sources:** [Honda Newsroom — American Honda Extends Warranties on Select Automatic Transmissions](https://hondanews.com/en-US/releases/release-8bbb4ec4b4a29f49054b06004c34c679-american-honda-extends-warranties-on-select-automatic-transmissions) (accessed 2026-05-15); [Automotive News — Honda extends transmission warranty](https://www.autonews.com/article/20020923/ANA/209230795/honda-extends-transmission-warranty/) (accessed 2026-05-15); [Honda Problems — 1999-2004 Honda Automatic Transmission Failures](https://www.hondaproblems.com/transmission-failure/) (accessed 2026-05-15).

### Significance

This is the **only manufacturer-initiated warranty extension specifically affecting H-series vehicles**. It demonstrates Honda's recognition of a systemic transmission defect but also reveals the limitations of the remedy:

- Only 2% affected, meaning 98% of owners received no benefit
- 1997–1998 Preludes with the same M6HA were excluded despite identical hardware
- Repair costs for failed M6HA transmissions reached up to $4,000
- Class action settlement provided additional compensation but required litigation

## Common Warranty Claims: Oil Consumption

### The Issue

Oil consumption is the **most frequently discussed reliability issue** across H-series enthusiast forums. The consensus among forum members and documented experiences points to several contributing factors:

| Factor | Description | Forum Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Piston ring design/fitment | Stock rings reportedly have gap alignment issues; ring gaps found "horribly out of alignment" on rebuilt engines at 113K miles | [PreludeOnline — Why do H22's burn oil?](https://www.preludeonline.com/threads/why-do-h22s-burn-oil.223412/) (accessed 2026-05-15) |
| FRM sleeve interaction | Carbon fiber-reinforced metal cylinder walls may contribute to accelerated ring wear; batch-to-batch variation in FRM application creates inconsistency between engines | [PreludeOnline — Why do H22's burn oil?](https://www.preludeonline.com/threads/why-do-h22s-burn-oil.223412/) (accessed 2026-05-15); [Honda-Tech — FRM liner and boost](https://honda-tech.com/forums/forced-induction-16/frm-liner-boost-2931033/) (accessed 2026-05-15) |
| PCV system design | Oil vapor recirculation through PCV contributes to oil consumption; fresh OEM PCV valve replacement at 10K intervals reportedly improves consumption significantly | [PreludeOnline — alphajesse post #19](https://www.preludeonline.com/threads/why-do-h22s-burn-oil.223412/#post-2737082) (accessed 2026-05-15) |
| VTEC engagement | Oil is used as a cooling mechanism for camshafts during VTEC operation; hard driving and frequent VTEC use increase consumption rates | [PreludeOnline — H22-Lude post #32](https://www.preludeonline.com/threads/why-do-h22s-burn-oil.223412/#post-2987014) (accessed 2026-05-15) |

### Reported Consumption Rates

Forum members reported widely varying oil consumption patterns:

| Owner Report | Consumption Rate | Conditions |
|---|---|---|
| Gerhard (H22A4→H22A swap) | 3 quarts per 7,000 miles (pre-swap H22A4); 0 quarts post-H22A swap | Normal driving |
| pIERCE (H22A4) | 5 quarts between 7,500-mile changes | Full synthetic Valvoline, minimal VTEC use |
| Xetenor (2001 SH) | 1 quart every 3–4K miles | Normal driving, visible smoke trail in VTEC |
| 98auto | 1 quart per 6,500 miles when not driven hard; higher with aggressive driving | Variable |
| slipknot95758 | 1 quart every 4K miles | Daily VTEC use |
| mwalker56@me.co (2001, 195K miles) | No oil burning between changes | Conservative driving, 3K oil change intervals |
| golfradict (2001 Type SH, 119K miles) | No oil burning | 3K oil change intervals |

### Warranty Claim Experience

**Critical finding: Dealership-reported threshold for warranty coverage.** Forum member BBYlude (#4) reported that their Honda dealer stated they would require **1 quart of oil consumption per 800 miles** before a warranty claim for piston ring replacement would be approved. This threshold is significantly more stringent than Honda's published specification.

Another forum member (junkyardninja, #12) cited a Honda specification stating the car can burn **1 quart in 3,000 miles and still be "in spec."** This discrepancy between published specification and dealer interpretation created a de facto barrier to warranty approval.

**Service bulletin recognition.** Honda issued service bulletin SB618958 referencing FRM sleeve honing procedures for oil consumption, indicating Honda acknowledged the issue at the dealer level. However, this was classified as a service bulletin, not a warranty extension, meaning:

- Owners had to bring vehicles to dealers voluntarily
- Coverage was at dealer discretion
- No formal notification mailings were sent to owners
- The issue was not classified as a safety defect

**Sources:** [PreludeOnline — Why do H22's burn oil?](https://www.preludeonline.com/threads/why-do-h22s-burn-oil.223412/) (accessed 2026-05-15); [PreludeOnline — The reason your 5th Gen burns oil](https://www.preludeonline.com/threads/the-reason-your-5th-gen-burns-oil-between-changes.252060/) (accessed 2026-05-15); [Honda-Tech — FRM Service Bulletin SB618958](https://honda-tech.com/forums/honda-prelude-4/honing-frm-sleeves-service-bulletin-sb618958-18939-a-405266/) (accessed 2026-05-15, Cloudflare-blocked).

## Common Warranty Claims: Timing Belt Tensioner

### The Issue

The H-series auto-tensioner (timing belt tensioner) was a known failure point. Unlike oil consumption, tensioner failure could cause catastrophic engine damage (valve-to-piston contact) if the belt jumped timing.

### Service Bulletin Recognition

Honda issued service bulletin **#01-010** recognizing auto-tensioner failure as a known issue. An NHTSA complaint documented a consumer who received this service bulletin acknowledgment from Honda. The complaint noted:

- Owner's manual recommended 105,000-mile inspection interval
- Tensioner failed at 79,000 miles
- Timing jump caused valve-to-piston damage
- Complete engine overhaul required ($2,300 cost to consumer)
- Honda did not issue a formal recall; covered under service bulletin

**Sources:** [Center for Auto Safety — 1998 Honda Prelude complaint database](https://www.autosafety.org/vehicle-safety-check/1998-honda-prelude/) (accessed 2026-05-15).

### Forum Discussion

Honda-Tech thread ["Stupid H22 Timing Belt Tensioner!!!!!"](https://honda-tech.com/forums/honda-prelude-4/stupid-h22-tensioner-2865102/) documents multiple owners experiencing tensioner failures, though the thread content is behind Cloudflare login protection. The thread title and existence indicate this was a recognized pain point among H-series owners.

**Sources:** [Honda-Tech — Stupid H22 Timing Belt Tensioner!!!!](https://honda-tech.com/forums/honda-prelude-4/stupid-h22-tensioner-2865102/) (accessed 2026-05-15, Cloudflare-blocked).

## Common Warranty Claims: Other Issues

### Ignition Switch (NHTSA Recalls 02V120000, 05V025000)

Two separate ignition switch recalls affected H-series vehicles. These were formal NHTSA recalls, not warranty claims:

| Recall | Year | Defect | Remedy |
|---|---|---|---|
| 02V120000 | May 2002 | Electrical contacts degraded; engine stalling | Dealer replaced ignition switch |
| 05V025000 | Jan 2005 | Interlock defect; key removable without shifting to Park | Dealer inspected and installed redesigned interlock lever (~402,525 vehicles) |

These recalls were free at dealerships and did not require warranty coverage.

### Lower Ball Joint (NHTSA 99V069000)

May 1999 recall for premature ball joint wear on 1997–1998 Preludes. Formal recall with dealer replacement. Not a warranty claim.

## Post-Warranty Goodwill Repairs

For H-series vehicles that fell outside warranty coverage, Honda offered **goodwill repairs** on a case-by-case basis:

- Owners could contact Honda Customer Service at (800) 999-1009 and request a "goodwill repair"
- Proof of following Honda's recommended maintenance schedule was typically required
- Honda might cover **50% of repair costs**, with some 2003 vehicle owners reporting up to 75% coverage
- This was discretionary and not guaranteed
- Most relevant for timing belt tensioner failures and oil consumption issues occurring just outside warranty

**Source:** [Honda Problems — 1999-2004 Honda Automatic Transmission Failures](https://www.hondaproblems.com/transmission-failure/) (accessed 2026-05-15).

## Key Findings

1. **Consistent warranty terms.** Honda's factory warranty (3yr/36K mi bumper-to-bumper, 5yr/60K mi powertrain) remained unchanged throughout the entire H-series production period (1991–2001). There were no mid-cycle warranty improvements or degradation.

2. **One transmission extension.** The only manufacturer-initiated warranty extension specifically affecting H-series vehicles was the 2002 automatic transmission extension covering 2000–2001 Prelude, Accord, and Odyssey models with M6HA transmissions. A 2006 class-action settlement extended this to 93 months/109,000 miles.

3. **Oil consumption was never formally recognized.** Despite being the most widely discussed H-series issue across enthusiast forums, oil consumption was never addressed through a formal warranty extension, recall, or customer notification campaign. Honda chose dealer-level service bulletins (SB618958) over formal warranty action.

4. **Warranty claim thresholds were opaque.** Forum evidence suggests dealers applied consumption thresholds (e.g., 1 quart/800 miles) that were more stringent than Honda's published specifications (1 quart/3,000 miles), creating uncertainty for warranty claim applicants.

5. **Timing belt gap.** The timing belt tensioner failure issue was recognized via service bulletin #01-010 but never elevated to a formal recall or warranty extension. The 105,000-mile inspection interval recommended by Honda fell outside the 5-year/60,000-mile powertrain warranty, leaving many owners responsible for catastrophic repair costs.

6. **Goodwill repairs as fallback.** For vehicles just outside warranty, Honda occasionally offered partial-cost goodwill repairs (typically 50%, sometimes up to 75%), but this was discretionary and required proof of maintenance compliance.

---

## Conflicts and Limitations

- **Dealer interpretation vs. published specs.** The discrepancy between Honda's published oil consumption specification (1 quart/3,000 miles) and the dealer-reported threshold (1 quart/800 miles) could not be independently verified without access to Honda's official service documentation. The forum reports are secondhand.
- **Cloudflare-blocked sources.** Several Honda-Tech forum threads containing relevant warranty claim discussions are behind Cloudflare login protection, preventing full content extraction.
- **No quantitative warranty claim data.** Honda does not publicly release warranty claim statistics by model or component. All claim frequency data is derived from self-reported forum posts and NHTSA complaint databases, which reflect reporting behavior rather than actual failure rates.
- **Transmission extension specifics unclear.** The exact extent of the 2002 transmission warranty extension (additional months/miles beyond the base warranty) is not fully documented in available sources. The 93-month/109,000-mile figure comes from the 2006 class-action settlement, which may or may not match the original extension terms.
- **International warranty terms unknown.** This research focuses on U.S./North American warranty terms. JDM and EDM warranty periods may have differed, but no authoritative sources were found for those markets.
