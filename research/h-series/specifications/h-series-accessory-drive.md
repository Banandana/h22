## Summary

The Honda H-series engine (H22A, H22A1, H22A4) uses a split accessory drive architecture: OBD1 (1992–1996) models employ two separate V-belts — one for the alternator and A/C compressor, another for the power steering pump — while OBD2 (1997–2001) models transition to a single 6-rib serpentine belt driving all three accessories plus the crankshaft. This document covers OEM part numbers, bracket designs, belt specifications, and cross-generation compatibility across all H-series variants from 1992–2001.

### Key Differences Between Generations

The 4th-gen and 5th-gen power steering pumps are **not directly interchangeable** due to bracket mounting differences. Forum members confirm that the 5th-gen PS bracket does not bolt up to the 4th-gen block without modification (cutting/welding required). The pump internals may be similar, but the external mounting flange and bracket interface differ.

## Bracket Design

### OBD1 Bracket Architecture (1992–1996)

The OBD1 H-series uses a **two-belt, dual-bracket** layout:

| Bracket | Location | Mounting | Notes |
|---------|----------|----------|-------|
| **Alternator bracket** | Front face, passenger side (right) | Through-bolt into block | Sliding adjustment slot for belt tension |
| **A/C compressor bracket** | Front face, driver side (left) | Bolted to block | Fixed position; no adjustment needed |
| **Power steering bracket** | Front face, driver side (left) | 3-bolt pattern specific to BB1 | Not interchangeable with BB6 bracket |

The alternator bracket uses a **sliding bracket with tensioner bolt** design: loosen the tensioner bolt, pivot the alternator to adjust belt tension, then retighten. The A/C compressor is fixed-position with no tensioning mechanism.

### OBD2 Bracket Architecture (1997–2001)

The OBD2 H-series transitions to a **single-serpentine-bracket** layout:

| Bracket | Location | Mounting | Notes |
|---------|----------|----------|-------|
| **PS pump bracket** | Front face, driver side | Part #56994-P5M-000 | Specific to 5th-gen; not interchangeable with 4th-gen |
| **Combined accessory rail** | Front face | Single casting | Integrates alternator, A/C, and PS mounting points |

The OBD2 design consolidates the accessory mounting into a more compact arrangement, with the single 6-rib serpentine belt replacing the two separate belts.

### Interchangeability Summary

| Component | 4th gen → 5th gen | 5th gen → 4th gen |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Alternator** | Compatible (same Denso type) | Compatible |

## Belt Routing and Specifications

### OBD1 Belt Configuration (1992–1996)

OBD1 H-series engines use **two separate V-belts**:

| Belt | Drives | Rib Count | Length | OEM Part Number |
|------|--------|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Alternator/A/C belt** | Alternator + A/C compressor | 5 ribs | 1,050 mm (41.3 in) | 38920-PK1-A04 |
| **Power steering belt** | Power steering pump only | 5 ribs | Separate length | Honda TechInfo (varies by year) |

The alternator/A/C belt routes around the crankshaft pulley (5-rib), alternator pulley, and A/C compressor pulley in a single loop. The power steering belt runs separately from the crankshaft to the PS pump pulley.

### OBD2 Belt Configuration (1997–2001)

OBD2 H-series engines use a **single 6-rib serpentine belt**:

| Belt | Drives | Rib Count | Length | OEM Part Number |
|------|--------|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| **Serpentine belt** | Crank → alternator → A/C → PS | 6 ribs | ~1,200 mm (est.) | Varies by application |

The serpentine belt follows a continuous path around the crankshaft pulley (now 6-rib), alternator pulley, A/C compressor pulley, power steering pump pulley, and an idler/tensioner pulley.

### Belt Routing Diagrams

#### OBD1 (Two-Belt Layout)

```
                    [Alternator]
                       /\
                      /  \  <-- 5-rib belt (38920-PK1-A04, 1050mm)
                     /    \
          [Crank] --/      \-- [A/C Compressor]
             |
             |  <-- 5-rib belt (separate, PS only)
             |
        [PS Pump]
```

#### OBD2 (Single Serpentine Layout)

```
              [Idler/Tensioner]
                  /   \
                 /     \
   [PS Pump] <--/       \--> [Alternator]
       |                         |
       |        SERPENTINE       |
       |        (6-rib)          |
       |                         |
   [Crank] <------------------ [A/C Compressor]
```

### AC Delete / Bypass Belt

For owners who remove the A/C compressor, an aftermarket bypass belt (NAPA NBH 25050345 or NBH 25040384) allows the alternator to run without the A/C pulley in the loop. This reduces parasitic drag by approximately 2–5 HP and eliminates the weight of the A/C compressor (~4.6–5.5 kg).

## Notes

### Conflicts and Discrepancies

1. **Crank pulley rib count:** One Facebook post claims "F22 pulleys are 6 rib and the H22 is 5 rib," while another forum member (red92s, Honda-Tech) states "The H22 crank pulley has 6 ribs instead of 5 like the F22/H23 crank pulley." These statements contradict each other. The Honda-Tech thread (multiple experienced builders) suggests OBD1 H22 = 5 ribs, OBD2 H22 = 6 ribs. The F22 appears to consistently use 6 ribs. This needs physical verification on actual engines.

2. **Belt length for OBD2:** The exact serpentine belt length for 1997–2001 H22A4 is not definitively documented in any source found. The 1,050 mm figure from the forum is for the OBD1 alternator/A/C belt, not the OBD2 serpentine. Additional research needed.

3. **A/C bracket interchangeability:** Multiple forum posts suggest A/C brackets are interchangeable between 4th and 5th gen, but no definitive part number cross-reference was found. The consensus is "they should work" but this is anecdotal.

### Open Questions

- Crank pulley rib count discrepancy between sources needs physical verification.
- Whether JDM H22A (Redtop) uses different PS/A/C brackets than USDM — not verified.
