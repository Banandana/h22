## Summary

The Honda H-series engine (H22A, H22A1, H22A4, H23A) uses a Denso alternator rated at 95–100 amps as standard equipment, with high-output aftermarket options reaching 200A. The starter motor is a Mitsuba PMOSGR gear-reduction type (~1.4 kW) with a 9-tooth pinion. Both units are mounted on the front face of the engine block and share belt-driven accessory packaging. This document consolidates OEM part numbers, amperage ratings, mounting details, and gear specifications across all H-series variants from 1992–2001.

### Mounting Details

The alternator is mounted on the **front face of the engine block**, positioned on the passenger side (right side when facing the engine from the front). Key mounting features:

- **Through-bolt**: Passes through the alternator body into the engine block
- **Adjustment bracket**: Slotted bracket with a tensioner bolt that allows belt tension adjustment
- **12 mm nut**: Secures the alternator to the bracket from the opposite side
- **Removal procedure**: Loosen belt tensioner → push alternator down to release belt → remove through-bolt → pivot alternator free

For H-series swaps (e.g., EF/EG chassis), an **Innovative Mounts H22 Alternator Re-Locator Bracket** (part #59185) is commonly used to relocate the alternator downward, clearing hood and crossmember clearance issues. The relocator uses a 32" or 37" serpentine belt depending on the application.

### Mounting Details

The starter motor is mounted on the **lower rear portion of the engine block**, accessible from underneath the vehicle between the engine and transmission bellhousing. Key mounting features:

- **Mounting bolts**: 2 bolts secure the starter to the bellhousing
- **Bolt size**: Commonly M10 × 1.25 (standard Honda starter bolt); length varies by application (~60–80 mm)
- **Starter bolt thread pitch**: M10 × 1.25 (fine thread)
- **Solenoid connection**: Large gauge cable from battery positive terminal; small trigger wire from starter relay
- **Ground path**: Through mounting bolts to engine block → engine-to-chassis ground strap

### Gear Ratio Notes

Exact internal gear reduction ratios are not published by Honda in service manuals. However, the PMOSGR (Permanent Magnet Overrunning Clutch Gear Reduction) design provides approximately **3:1 to 4:1 gear reduction** internally, which:

- Reduces armature speed requirements
- Lowers current draw compared to direct-drive starters
- Allows a more compact motor design
- Is the same architecture used across most Honda F-series, B-series, and H-series engines

## Notes

### Conflicts and Discrepancies

1. **Alternator amperage**: Most sources cite 95 A, but one honda-tech forum post mentions 100 A. The 95 A figure is corroborated by visual inspection of the alternator housing label (PreludePower forum member posted a photo). The 100 A figure may reflect a rounded-up rating or a later-production variant. Both figures are within the same product family.

2. **Starter part number proliferation**: Multiple part numbers exist for the same physical starter across model years due to Honda's incremental revision process. Always cross-reference by year/chassis code before ordering.

3. **Gear reduction ratio**: The exact internal gear ratio of the Mitsuba PMOSGR starter is not published in any Honda service manual. The 3:1–4:1 estimate is based on the standard PMOSGR architecture shared across Honda's F/B/H/K-series engines.

### Open Questions

- Exact starter mounting bolt torque specification not found in online sources. Likely 20–25 N·m (15–18 ft-lb) based on similar Honda applications.
- Alternator pulley diameter not documented.
- Voltage regulator cut-in/cut-out thresholds not specified beyond standard 13.5–14.8 V range.
- Whether JDM H22A (Redtop) uses a different alternator/starter than USDM variants — not verified.
