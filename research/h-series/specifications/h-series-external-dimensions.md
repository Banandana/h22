## Summary

This document covers the complete external dimensional profile of the Honda H-series engine family (H22A, H22A1, H22A4, H23A), including core block/head dimensions, fully accessorized envelope dimensions, and accessory packaging details. The H-series is a transverse-mounted DOHC inline-4 designed for the Honda Prelude and Accord chassis. Core engine measurements come from both community-measured data (Honda-Tech forum, 2007) and compiled reference sources (QWEN.md, Wikipedia, wikimotors). The most reliable single measurement set comes from Honda-Tech user Acidcrakker who physically measured an H23 on 2007-12-10, noting the H22 is "probably an inch or two taller." The block height alone is 219.5 mm (8.64 in), confirming the engine's substantial vertical footprint. Accessory packaging adds significant width due to the transverse-mount layout with alternator, starter, power steering pump, and A/C compressor mounted on the front/side of the engine.

## Accessory Packaging Details

### Transverse-Mount Layout

The H-series is designed for transverse mounting (crankshaft axis perpendicular to vehicle longitudinal axis), which places accessories on the front and side of the engine rather than the traditional longitudinal-mount layout.

## Notes

### Conflicts and Discrepancies

1. **Dimension set inconsistency:** Two different measurement sets exist:
   - QWEN.md cites "22" × 22" × 12"" — explicitly labeled as H23 measurements
   - Honda-Tech Acidcrakker measured "22 × 22 × 12" — also H23
   - The T-084 file cites "20.5" × 14.0" × 21.0"" — attributed to H23 but labeled differently
   - These are clearly the same physical engine measured from different reference planes. The 22" × 22" × 12" set likely measures the entire engine assembly including accessories, while the 20.5" × 14" × 21" set measures the core block/head.

2. **H22 vs H23 dimensional differences:** No quantitative delta published. Acidcrakker qualitatively stated "the H22 is probably an inch or two taller" than the H23, but this contradicts the fact that the H22 has a shorter stroke (90.7 mm vs 95 mm) and identical block height (219.5 mm). The difference would more likely be in the oil pan depth and crankshaft counterweight radius. This discrepancy should be flagged for future verification.

3. **Accessory dimensions:** No published OEM dimensional data exists for H-series accessory packaging. All figures are estimates derived from component part numbers and cross-referenced with similar Honda engines. The OnAllCylinders article provides methodology but no H-series-specific data.

4. **Hood clearance:** Some swap guides report hood contact issues when installing H22 into EG/EK chassis, but no precise clearance measurements are published. This is anecdotal evidence from the community.

### Assumptions Made

1. Bore spacing assumed at 98.0 mm based on Honda inline-4 standard (shared F/H-series architecture)
2. Accessory belt drive configuration inferred from OBD1/OBD2 service manual references
3. H22 vs H23 dimensional delta estimated qualitatively; no measured data exists
4. Engine bay clearance figures derived from swap guide anecdotes, not factory specifications

### Open Questions

1. **Exact fully accessorized dimensions:** No published OEM dimensional drawing exists for the H-series with all accessories installed.
2. **H22 vs H23 precise dimensional delta:** Qualitative only ("1-2 inches taller"); needs measured confirmation.
3. **Accessory belt routing diagrams:** Available in service manuals but not digitized in current research.
