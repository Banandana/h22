## Summary

This document covers the complete flywheel and clutch specifications for the Honda H-series engine family (H22A, H22A1, H22A4, H23A) across all applications — Prelude BB1/BB2/BB6, Accord CH1, Acura CL, and Torneo. The H-series uses a single dry-plate organic clutch with a steel flywheel, shared across all variants due to their common architecture. Key specifications include a 219mm OEM clutch disc diameter, 24-spline input shaft, 76 ft-lb flywheel bolt torque, and 19 ft-lb pressure plate bolt torque. Aftermarket lightweight flywheels range from 9 lbs (Competition Clutch ultra-light) to 12.6 lbs (ACT StreetLite), compared to approximately 15-18 lbs stock. The pilot bearing is Honda part number 91006-634-008, used across B/H/F-series applications.

## Installation Torque Sequence

Both flywheel bolts and pressure plate bolts follow the same tightening procedure:

| Step | Action | Detail |
|------|--------|--------|
| **1** | Clean mating surfaces | Ensure flywheel and pressure plate faces are clean and flat |
| **2** | Install clutch disc | Use Honda alignment tool (included in Exedy KHC03 kit) to center disc |
| **3** | Install pressure plate | Align using tool; hand-tighten all 6 bolts |
| **4** | Torque pattern | 180° alternating: 12 → 6 → 2 → 8 → 4 → 10 o'clock positions |
| **5** | Final torque (pressure plate) | 19 ft-lb per bolt |
| **6** | Torque pattern (flywheel) | Same 180° alternating sequence |
| **7** | Final torque (flywheel) | 76 ft-lb per bolt |
| **8** | Verify alignment | Remove alignment tool; rotate crank by hand to verify free movement |

## Notes

### Conflicts and Discrepancies

1. **OEM flywheel weight:** No Honda service manual publishes an exact flywheel weight. The ~15-18 lb estimate is derived from: (a) JUN AUTO listing stock weight as 8.4 kg (18.5 lbs) for comparison against their 5.2 kg (11.5 lb) lightweight unit, and (b) Honda-Tech kb58 noting that an aluminum flywheel saved ~15 lbs off a 475 lb complete assembly. These two data points bracket the likely OEM weight at 15-18 lbs. This is the most significant gap in the data — no official figure exists.

2. **OEM clutch disc diameter:** There is some ambiguity between sources. Real Street Performance lists the KHC03 OEM disc as 219mm (8-5/8"), while Exedy USA lists their H-series Stage 1 (08806) as 240mm. The 219mm figure appears to be the true OEM specification, while the 240mm is an Exedy Stage 1 upsized disc. This is confirmed by the fact that multiple aftermarket manufacturers list ~219mm as the baseline H-series disc size.

3. **Exedy torque capacity discrepancy:** Exedy USA lists Stage 1 (08806) at 368 ft-lbs but Stage 2 (08952) at only 220 ft-lbs. This seems counterintuitive — Stage 2 is typically higher performance. The explanation is that the Stage 1 rating (368 ft-lbs) is for the upsized 240mm disc with higher clamp load, while the Stage 2 (220-221 ft-lbs) uses the stock 219mm diameter with cerametallic 4-puck design. The larger disc area of Stage 1 gives it a higher theoretical torque capacity despite lower friction coefficient.

4. **JUN Lightweight vs Ultra Light weights:** Both JUN AUTO variants (2010M-H006 Lightweight and Ultra Light) list the same weight of 5.2 kg (11.5 lbs) on Black Hawk Japan's product page. This may be an error in the listing, or JUN may differentiate them by material thickness rather than weight. The distinction is unclear.

5. **Ring gear tooth count:** Not found in any accessible source. Tilton Engineering catalog does not list H-series flywheels (they carry B-series and K-series but not H-series specific flywheels). The tooth count is needed for starter motor compatibility but was not discovered through web research.

6. **Flywheel outer diameter:** Not explicitly published in any source. Honda-Tech thread "Need a couple dimensions of an H22 flywheel..." exists but content requires login. The clutch disc diameter (219mm) provides a minimum bound — the flywheel must be at least this diameter plus clearance for the friction surface step.

### Assumptions Made

1. F-series (F22A/F22B/F23A) flywheel and clutch components are identical to H-series due to shared architecture — confirmed by ACT, Competition Clutch, and Exedy listing both H-series and F-series under the same part numbers.
2. All H-series variants use the same 24-spline input shaft — confirmed by Exedy USA and multiple aftermarket clutch listings.
3. The 76 ft-lb flywheel torque and 19 ft-lb pressure plate torque are consistent across all H-series variants — sourced from Helms Manual cited for H22/H23 applications, which share the same bolt patterns.
4. Pilot bearing 91006-634-008 is shared across B/H/F/K-series — confirmed by eBay listings and HondaPartsNow fitment data showing compatibility with B16, B18, D16, K20 applications.

### Open Questions

1. **Exact OEM flywheel weight:** Not published in any Honda service manual. Estimated 15-18 lbs based on aftermarket comparison data.
2. **Flywheel outer diameter:** Not found in any accessible source.
3. **Ring gear tooth count:** Not found in any accessible source. Needed for starter motor selection.
4. **Flywheel bolt thread size:** Likely M10 or M12 based on ARP 208-2802 product, but exact specification not confirmed.
5. **H22A vs H23A flywheel differences:** Sources list both under the same part numbers, suggesting interchangeability, but no explicit confirmation found.

**Key takeaway:** The H-series and F-series share nearly identical flywheel and clutch hardware. The B-series uses different spline counts and smaller disc diameters. The K-series uses entirely different hardware.
