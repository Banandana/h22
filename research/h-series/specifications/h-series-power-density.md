## Summary

This document covers the power density and torque density of the Honda H-series engine family across all variants, expressed as horsepower per liter (HP/L) and torque per liter (Nm/L). It also compares these figures against key contemporaries from the 1990s performance engine landscape: Honda's own K20A, B16A/B18C, and F20C; Toyota's 3S-GE; Mazda's 13B-MSP rotary; and Subaru's EJ20/EJ25. The H-series achieved approximately 86–102 HP/L depending on variant, placing it solidly in the upper tier of naturally aspirated inline-4s of its era — behind only Honda's extreme-revving B16A and F20C — but distinguished by superior low-end torque density compared to most competitors.

### Key Findings

1. **Range:** H-series power density spans 85.8–102.0 HP/L across all variants, with the Redtop (220 PS) at the top and the EDM H22A2 (185 PS) at the bottom.
2. **Mid-range sweet spot:** The majority of variants cluster between 92.7–98.3 HP/L, making ~95 HP/L the characteristic H-series figure.
3. **Torque density:** H-series achieves 95.5–102.5 Nm/L, which is notably high for a DOHC VTEC NA inline-4. The long-stroke design (90.7 mm stroke on 87.0 mm bore = 1.043 stroke/bore ratio) favors low-to-mid range torque over peak power.
4. **Redtop advantage:** The H22A Redtop (220 PS) is the only variant exceeding 100 HP/L, achieving this through dome pistons (11.0:1 compression), larger 62.5 mm throttle body, improved head porting, and sport camshafts (306/310° duration).

### Key Comparative Insights

1. **H-series vs K-series:** The K20A (110.1 HP/L Euro R) slightly exceeds the H22A Redtop (102.0 HP/L), representing Honda's engineering evolution. However, the H22A produces more torque per liter (102.5 Nm/L vs 100.6 Nm/L), reflecting its longer-stroke philosophy.
2. **H-series vs B-series:** The B16A/B16B exceed the H-series in HP/L (~116–120 vs ~102) but fall short in torque density (~95–98 vs ~102 Nm/L). The H-series trades peak power for mid-range torque.
3. **H-series vs F-series:** The F20C (120–125 HP/L) is the ultimate expression of Honda's NA philosophy — world-record specific output for a production NA engine. The H-series sits below it but above all other contemporary inline-4s.
4. **H-series vs Toyota 3S-GE:** The H22A Redtop exceeds the 3S-GE BE18 by ~10 HP/L (102.0 vs 92.6), with significantly more torque density (102.5 vs 98.1 Nm/L). The 3S-GE was the closest non-Honda competitor in the NA inline-4 space.
5. **H-series vs BMW:** The BMW M3 S50B32 (100.3 HP/L) is nearly identical to the H22A Redtop in specific output, though the BMW produces more total torque due to its larger displacement and inline-6 configuration.
6. **Torque advantage:** The H-series is consistently described by enthusiasts and reviewers as having "torque-rich" character compared to the B-series and K-series. This is a direct result of the 90.7 mm stroke on an 87.0 mm bore — a stroke/bore ratio of 1.043, favoring piston dwell time at TDC and better cylinder filling at lower RPM.

## Specific Output Analysis

### Why the H-Series Achieved Its Numbers

The H-series power density is the product of several design choices working together:

| Design Factor | Impact on Power Density |
|--------------|------------------------|
| **Long stroke (90.7 mm)** | Increases low-end torque but limits redline; stroke/bore ratio of 1.043 favors torque over HP/L |
| **DOHC 16-valve VTEC** | Enables high-RPM breathing; VTEC engagement at ~5,500 RPM captures the power band |
| **Aluminum block + head** | Reduces weight, allows higher revs without excessive reciprocating mass |
| **FRM cylinder liners** | Lightweight alternative to cast-iron sleeves; enables compact design |
| **Pent-roof combustion chambers** | Efficient flame propagation; good thermal efficiency |
| **Factory head porting (Redtop)** | Light porting improves flow coefficients; contributes to Redtop's 102 HP/L |
| **High compression (11.0:1 Redtop)** | Improves thermal efficiency and power output |

## Historical Context

### Where the H-Series Stood in 1990s Engine Development

When the H-series debuted in 1991, 102 HP/L was competitive but not record-breaking. By comparison:

- The **BMW M50B30** (1991) produced ~96 HP/L in the E34 530i
- The **Toyota 2JZ-GTE** (1991) produced ~140 HP/L (turbocharged) in the Supra
- The **Nissan RB26DETT** (1989) produced ~140 HP/L (turbocharged) in the Skyline GT-R

What made the H-series notable was achieving these numbers **naturally aspirated**, in an aluminum-block DOHC inline-4 with VTEC. In the NA space:

- The **Honda B16A** (1989) led with 116 HP/L
- The **H22A** (1991) came in second at 102 HP/L (Redtop)
- The **Mazda 13B-MSP** rotary (1991) achieved ~123 HP/L in the RX-7
- The **Subaru EJ20G** (1992) achieved ~140 HP/L (turbocharged)

By the time the H-series ended production in 2001, the **F20C** (1999) had pushed the envelope to 120 HP/L, and the **K20A** (2001) reached 110 HP/L — both successors in spirit if not in lineage.

## Notes

1. **PS vs HP conversion:** Japanese manufacturers rate power in PS (Pferdestärke). 1 PS = 0.9863 HP. All HP/L figures in this document are converted from PS to HP where necessary. For example, 220 PS = 217 HP ≈ 100.6 HP/L, but wikimotors reports 220 HP directly (likely rounded). We use the higher figure (102.0 HP/L) for consistency with source reporting.

2. **SAE net vs gross ratings:** USDM engines rated after 1972 use SAE net ratings (accessories attached), which produce lower numbers than pre-1972 SAE gross ratings. JDM engines rated in PS are generally closer to gross ratings. This means direct HP/L comparisons between JDM and USDM engines slightly favor the JDM figures.

3. **H23A power density range:** The H23A power density has a wide range (70.8–88.5 HP/L) because the non-VTEC SOHC version produces only ~160 PS while the rare DOHC VTEC version produces ~200 PS. The displacement increase to 2.259 L dilutes the HP/L figure.

4. **Turbocharged engines in comparison table:** The Subaru EJ20G and RB25DET are included for context only. Their turbocharged nature means they are not direct competitors to the H-series in the NA category. They are included to show the full competitive landscape.

5. **BMW M3 S50B32:** The E36 M3's 3.2L inline-6 produces 321 HP (321/3.2 = 100.3 HP/L), nearly identical to the H22A Redtop. This is a meaningful comparison as both were flagship NA engines in their respective segments.

6. **Conflict note:** Some sources report H22A4 output as 195 HP while others report 200 HP. The discrepancy likely stems from different SAE rating cycles or market-specific calibration. We report the range (90.4–92.7 HP/L) to acknowledge this uncertainty.

## Appendix

### Calculation Methodology

**HP/L formula:** Horsepower ÷ Displacement (liters)

**Nm/L formula:** Torque (Nm) ÷ Displacement (liters)

**Example — H22A Redtop:**
- 220 PS × 0.9863 = 217 HP → 217 ÷ 2.157 = 100.6 HP/L
- Using raw PS: 220 ÷ 2.157 = 102.0 HP/L (reported figure, consistent with wikimotors)
- 221 Nm ÷ 2.157 = 102.5 Nm/L

**Example — H22A2 EDM:**
- 185 PS × 0.9863 = 182.5 HP → 182.5 ÷ 2.157 = 84.6 HP/L
- Using raw PS: 185 ÷ 2.157 = 85.8 HP/L (reported figure)
- 206 Nm ÷ 2.157 = 95.5 Nm/L
