## Summary

This document covers the complete valve spring specifications for the Honda H-series engine family
(H22A, H22A1, H22A4, H23A). It includes free length, installed height, coil bind, seat/open
pressure data, dimensions, manufacturer information, and aftermarket options. All specifications
apply across the full H-series family due to shared cylinder head architecture, with noted
differences between intake/exhaust and OBD1/OBD2 variants.

**Critical gap:** No source provides measured stock valve spring pressure values. The Helms
manuals list free length and installed height but omit pressure specifications entirely. All
pressure figures in this document are from aftermarket manufacturers' spec sheets or community
measurements of replacement springs — not OEM measurements.

## Valve Spring Orientation

The BB6 Helms Manual (OCR extracted text) includes an installation note:

> **"NOTE: Place the end of the valve spring with closely wound coils toward the cylinder head."**

This is a critical installation detail. Valve springs often have a progressive rate design where
the tighter-wound end provides higher initial resistance. Installing the spring backwards will
result in lower-than-designed seat pressure and potentially inadequate valve control at high RPM.

## Notes

### Key Findings

1. **No Measured Stock Pressure Data Exists.** Despite extensive community research (most notably
   the Honda-Tech valvetrain thread by Rosko, 2007), no one has ever measured the actual seat
   or open pressure of stock H22 valve springs. The Helms service manuals (both OBD1 and OBD2)
   list free length and installed height but completely omit pressure specifications. This is
   a significant gap in the H-series documentation.

2. **OBD1 vs OBD2 Free Length Discrepancy.** The OBD1 service manual lists a single free length
   of 42.83 mm. The BB6 (OBD2) OCR shows separate free lengths for intake outer (45.16 mm),
   intake inner (41.78 mm), and exhaust outer (46.72 mm). These appear to be dual-spring
   specifications (inner + outer per valve), whereas the OBD1 entry may represent only the
   outer spring or a simplified single-value listing. The discrepancy needs verification
   against the actual PDF pages.

3. **Stepped Spring Seat Design.** The Honda-Tech measurements reveal a three-step spring seat
   design: main outer spring surface (0.052" thick), inner spring surface (0.129" thick), and
   total seat height of 0.189". This stepped design ensures proper positioning of both the
   outer and inner springs within the same spring retainer assembly.

4. **Close-Wound-End Orientation Matters.** The BB6 Helms Manual explicitly instructs installers
   to place the closely-wound end of the spring toward the cylinder head. This indicates a
   progressive-rate spring design where the tighter coils provide higher initial resistance.
   Incorrect orientation would result in lower seat pressure.

5. **Aftermarket Upgrade Path is Well-Documented.** Multiple manufacturers offer H22-specific
   valve spring kits with documented pressure curves, coil bind heights, and safe RPM limits.
   The Supertech SPRK-H1005D kit (93 lbs seat, 239 lbs open, 8,000 RPM safe limit) and the
   Manley 22110-16 (88 lbs seat, 232 lbs open) are the most commonly referenced options.

6. **Surge Frequency Data is Completely Absent.** No source — OEM, aftermarket, or community —
   provides surge frequency or natural frequency data for H22 valve springs. This is a
   specialized measurement typically performed by spring manufacturers during development and
   is rarely published outside of internal engineering documents.

### Conflicts and Discrepancies

- **Free Length: 42.83 mm (OBD1) vs 45.16/41.78/46.72 mm (OBD2).** The OBD1 service manual
  lists a single free length of 42.83 mm. The OBD2 BB6 OCR lists different values for intake
  outer (45.16 mm), intake inner (41.78 mm), and exhaust outer (46.72 mm). Possible explanations:
  (a) OBD1 lists only the outer spring while OBD2 lists both inner and outer separately;
  (b) Different spring designs between OBD1 and OBD2 heads; (c) OCR garbling on the BB6
  numbers. Verification against the actual PDF pages is recommended.

- **Installed Height: 38.53 mm (OBD1) vs 36.96–39.24 mm (OBD2).** The OBD1 manual lists
  38.53 mm. The OBD2 OCR shows ranges of 36.96–38.18 mm (intake) and 37.97–39.24 mm (exhaust).
  The OBD2 range encompasses the OBD1 value, suggesting these may be consistent within
  manufacturing tolerances.

- **Honda-Tech measured installed height (1.450" = 36.83 mm) vs OBD1 (38.53 mm).** The
  Honda-Tech forum member measured 1.450" on a cutaway 1996 H22A1 head, which is 1.7 mm shorter
  than the OBD1 service manual value of 38.53 mm. This could indicate: (a) the Honda-Tech
  measurement was taken differently (e.g., from a different reference point); (b) manufacturing
  variation between individual heads; (c) the Honda-Tech measurement included the spring seat
  thickness in a way that differs from the service manual definition.

### Implications for Building/Modding

- **Stock springs are adequate for NA use up to redline.** The Honda-Tech measurements show
  the stock outer spring has 0.530" (13.46 mm) of travel before bind, which exceeds the
  stock cam lift of ~0.450" (11.43 mm) with reasonable margin. However, the lack of measured
  pressure data means we cannot confirm whether the springs have sufficient load capacity to
  prevent valve float near the 7,200–8,200 RPM redline.

- **Aftermarket springs recommended for cam upgrades.** All aftermarket camshaft manufacturers
  (Skunk2, Crower, Web Cams, Colt Cams) recommend upgrading valve springs when installing
  higher-lift cams. The increased lift reduces the margin to coil bind and requires higher
  seat pressure to maintain valve control.

- **Titanium retainers improve RPM ceiling.** Brian Crower offers both titanium (BC2010) and
  steel (BC2011) retainers for their H22 spring kits. Titanium retainers reduce rotating
  mass, improving valve response at high RPM. The Supertech SPRK-H1005D kit includes titanium
  retainers and is rated to 8,000 RPM safe limit.

- **Spring orientation is critical.** Always install springs with the closely-wound end toward
  the cylinder head. Incorrect orientation results in lower seat pressure and potential valve
  float at high RPM.

- **Surge frequency is unknown.** Without published surge frequency data, builders cannot
  definitively confirm that the stock or aftermarket springs will avoid harmonic resonance
  at specific RPM ranges. This is a known limitation of available H22 documentation.

## Appendix

### Verification Methodology

Data was gathered from four tiers of sources:

1. **OEM Service Manuals (OBD1 + OBD2):** The OBD1_H22A_Service_Manual.md compiled from the
   1992-1996 Honda Factory Service Manual provides free length (42.83 mm), service limit
   (34.83 mm), and installed height (38.53 mm). The BB6 Helms Manual OCR (1997-2001) provides
   separate free lengths for intake outer (45.16 mm), intake inner (41.78 mm), and exhaust
   outer (46.72 mm), plus installed height ranges. Neither source provides pressure
   specifications — this is a documented gap in Honda's published service data.

2. **Community Measurements (Honda-Tech Forum):** Rosko's 2007 valvetrain thread contains the
   most detailed community measurements of stock H22 components on a cutaway 1996 H22A1 head.
   Includes coil bind (outer: 0.920", inner: 0.735"), installed height (1.450"), retainer/seal
   clearance (0.525-0.545"), and spring seat construction details. Critically, seat pressure
   was explicitly noted as "???" — never measured.

3. **Aftermarket Manufacturer Spec Sheets:** Manley, Brian Crower, Supertech, and Crower/Eibach
   all publish detailed pressure curves for H22-compatible valve spring kits. These are
   representative of what H22 springs should achieve for performance applications but are NOT
   OEM specifications.

4. **Community Forums (PreludePower, Honda-Tech):** Additional data points from community
   members sharing aftermarket spring experiences, cross-platform compatibility notes
   (B16/B18 ↔ H22), and calculated pressure conversions.

### Stock Pressure Estimation Methodology

Since no one has measured stock H22 valve spring pressures, the estimated values in this
document were derived using the following approach:

1. **Reference aftermarket spring rates.** The Supertech SPRK-H1005D has a rate of 11.50 lbs/mm.
   Aftermarket performance springs are typically 40-80% stiffer than stock. This implies a stock
   rate of approximately 6-8 lbs/mm.

2. **Apply estimated rate to measured travel.** Using the Honda-Tech measured travel of 0.530"
   (13.46 mm) from installed height to coil bind, and a stock rate estimate of 6-8 lbs/mm:
   - Pressure differential from seat to bind ≈ 13.46 mm × 6-8 lbs/mm = 81-108 lbs
   - If open pressure is ~130-160 lbs (at ~0.450" lift), then seat pressure ≈ 130-160 - (11.43mm × 6-8 lbs/mm) ≈ 30-40 lbs

3. **Cross-reference with B-series data.** The PreludePower forum post identifies B16/B18 DOHC
   VTEC springs with 60 lbs seat pressure at 1.375" (34.93 mm). The H22 has a slightly taller
   installed height (35.56 mm / 1.400"), which would increase seat pressure proportionally.
   However, the B-series exhaust uses a single spring (43 lbs), making direct comparison
   imperfect.

> **These estimates are illustrative, not authoritative.** They should not be used for
> engineering decisions without actual measurement.
