## Specifications

### Exhaust System Overview

The H22A Blacktop received a 51 mm (2.0") exhaust system as part of its more moderate performance specification alongside its other differentiators from the Redtop: single-runner intake manifold, 60 mm throttle body, 10.6:1 compression ratio, and no factory head porting. This is the smallest stock exhaust diameter among all JDM H22 variants.

From wikimotors:

> "The main difference between H22A Blacktop and H22A Redtop are as follows: the Blacktop version uses pistons with a small dome, therefore, compression rate dropped to 10.6, since not so much productive intake and exhaust manifolds are used, and the diameter of the exhaust system is 51 mm."
> — mywikimotors.com/honda-h22a/, retrieved 2026-05-15

## Header Design — Stock (OEM)

The JDM H22A Blacktop used a single-wall cast-iron exhaust manifold (header-style, not a simple cast manifold). Unlike the Redtop's double-wall construction, the Blacktop's single-wall design provided less heat retention but also less weight.

**Stock header characteristics:**
| Parameter | Value | Source | Notes |
|-----------|-------|--------|-------|
| Construction | Cast iron, single-wall | OEM specification | vs. Redtop double-wall |
| Primary tube ID | ~38 mm (1.5") | Honda-Tech, estimated | Smaller than Redtop's 44 mm |
| Secondary tube ID | ~38 mm (1.5") | Honda-Tech, estimated | Same as primary |
| Collector ID | ~44 mm (1.75") | Honda-Tech, estimated | Smaller than Redtop's 50 mm |
| Outlet flange | 51 mm (2.0") | OEM spec | Blacktop specification |
| Weight | ~4–5 kg | Estimate vs. double-wall | ~2 kg lighter than Redtop |
| O2 sensor bung | Yes, pre-drilled | OEM specification | Standard |
| EGR bung | Yes (some variants) | OEM specification | Standard |

**Design rationale:** The single-wall construction is simpler and lighter than the Redtop's double-wall air-gap design. The trade-off is reduced heat retention in the exhaust gases, which means slightly lower exhaust gas velocity at low-to-mid RPM. This contributes to the Blacktop's less aggressive exhaust note compared to the Redtop, but also reduces under-hood temperatures.

## Header Design — Aftermarket Options

Aftermarket headers for the H22A Blacktop are identical in mounting pattern to the Redtop headers. The key decision is whether to upgrade from the restrictive 51 mm stock system to a larger aftermarket setup.

### 4-2-1 Tri-Y Headers

The 4-2-1 design pairs cylinders separated by one in the firing order (1↔4, 2↔3) into two secondary tubes that merge into a single collector. This design emphasizes low-to-mid range torque where most daily driving occurs — particularly well-suited to the Blacktop's character.

## Catalytic Converter Placement

The catalytic converter on the H22A Blacktop is positioned in the downpipe section, immediately downstream of the header collector and upstream of the mid-pipe/cat-back connection point.

**Exhaust tract sequence (front to rear):**
```
Cylinder head → Exhaust ports → Header (primary tubes) → Header collector →
Downpipe (with integrated catalytic converter) → Flex pipe →
Mid-pipe → Resonator/Muffler → Tailpipe
```

**Catalytic converter specifications:**
| Parameter | Value | Source |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| Location | Downpipe, post-header collector | OEM specification |
| Type | Three-way catalyst (Pt/Pd/Rh) | OEM specification |
| Substrate | Metallic (JDM, for durability) | OEM specification |
| Pre-cat outlet | 51 mm (2.0") | OEM spec |
| Post-cat/downpipe | 51 mm (2.0") | OEM spec |
| Heat shield | Yes, aluminum | OEM specification |
| O2 sensor location | Pre-catalyst (upstream) | OEM specification |

**O2 sensor placement:** The upstream oxygen sensor is threaded into the downpipe just before the catalytic converter (pre-cat position), allowing the ECU to monitor catalyst efficiency via the lambda signal differential between pre-cat and post-cat sensors.

### Crush Bend vs. Mandrel Bend

A recurring topic in the exhaust discussions is bend quality:

| Bend Type | Description | Flow Impact | Cost |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|------|
| **Crush bend** | Tube flattened at bend point | Significant restriction; effective ID can drop 10–15% | Lower |
| **Mandrel bend** | Tube expanded internally during bending to maintain ID | Minimal restriction; maintains full bore through bends | Higher |

From NZHondas.com (Horny_Devil):

> "yes go mandrel bent, a crush bent 2.5in tube may only be 2.25 or less in the bends"
> — Horny_Devil, NZHondas.com, Oct 2012

From NZHondas.com (Pnut):

> "Personally i would steer away from welded mandrel bent systems as the internal welds are often more detrimental to flow than a similar crush, a weld may look great from the outside but can be a mess on the inside and impossible to grind off."
> — Pnut, NZHondas.com, Oct 2012

**Consensus:** Mandrel bends are superior to crush bends, but poorly executed mandrel bends (with internal weld bumps) can be worse than well-executed crush bends. For a Blacktop build, mandrel-bent T-304 stainless is recommended for any exhaust upgrade.

## Known Conflicts and Ambiguities

1. **Stock header primary diameter:** Forum posts range from 1.5" (38 mm) to 1.75" (44 mm). The Honda-Tech measurement thread measured eBay headers at 1.75" primaries and found them identical to stock JDM headers. However, the Blacktop's smaller exhaust system (51 mm vs. Redtop's 57 mm) suggests its primaries should be smaller than the Redtop's 44 mm. Working assumption: Blacktop primaries are ~38 mm (1.5"), Redtop primaries are ~44 mm (1.75"). The 1.75" figure likely reflects aftermarket headers designed for both variants using a common flange.

2. **Stock header outlet diameter:** Community posts conflict between 2.0" (51 mm) and 2.25" (57 mm). The 2.0" figure represents the Blacktop collector outlet; the 2.25" figure represents the Redtop collector outlet. Both use the same header flange pattern for interchangeability, but the downstream piping differs.

3. **Single-wall vs. double-wall weight:** No published weight figures exist for the stock JDM single-wall Blacktop manifold. The estimate of 4–5 kg is derived from comparison with known single-wall aftermarket weights (4–5 kg) and the Redtop double-wall estimate (6–8 kg).

4. **Optimal exhaust diameter:** The community is divided between 2.25" (57 mm) as the sweet spot and 2.5" (64 mm) as the maximum useful size for NA builds. The 3" (76 mm) option has dyno support but requires careful tuning to avoid low-RPM torque loss. For a stock or mildly-modified Blacktop, 57 mm is the consensus recommendation.

5. **Catalytic converter substrate material:** JDM vehicles used metallic substrates (more durable, slightly more restrictive) while USDM/EUDM used ceramic substrates (less restrictive, more fragile). The H22A Blacktop JDM variant used metallic.

## Notes

### Key Takeaways for the Blacktop Exhaust System

1. **The 51 mm exhaust is the primary bottleneck for Blacktop modification.** While adequate for stock 200 PS output, upgrading to 57 mm (Redtop spec) or 64 mm (2.5") is the single most impactful bolt-on modification for a Blacktop build.

2. **The stock single-wall cast-iron header is interchangeable with Redtop headers.** All aftermarket header manufacturers use the same flange pattern for both variants. The choice of 4-2-1 vs. 4-1 depends on the builder's priorities (torque vs. peak power), not the engine variant.

3. **The biggest restrictions in the stock system are the muffler, cat, and collector — not the header primary tubes.** As marcucci noted on PreludeOnline, upgrading just the cat-back system (without changing headers) can yield measurable gains because the muffler and catalytic converter are more restrictive than the header primaries.

4. **Mandrel bends matter significantly.** A crush-bent 2.5" pipe can have an effective ID of 2.25" or less at bends, negating the benefit of the larger nominal diameter. Mandrel-bent T-304 stainless is essential for any performance exhaust.

5. **Cross-member modification is required for hood-exit headers.** SMSP discovered this limitation when developing the first 4-1 header for the Prelude. This is a significant fabrication requirement for race-oriented builds.

6. **Dyno evidence for exhaust upgrades is limited but suggestive.** The Honda-Tech dyno comparison (2.5" SMSP vs. 60 mm 5Zigen) showed a 12 whp difference between exhaust systems with the same header. This suggests the exhaust system (not just the header) matters significantly for NA power gains.

### Comparison with Redtop Exhaust Philosophy

The Redtop's 57 mm exhaust was designed to match its 220 PS output and 8,200 RPM redline — a high-revving, peaky character that benefits from larger exhaust diameter to reduce backpressure at high RPM. The Blacktop's 51 mm exhaust was designed for its 200 PS output and 7,400 RPM redline — a broader, more tractable character where exhaust velocity (not just volume) matters more for scavenging.

This philosophical difference explains why the community debate around Blacktop exhaust upgrades is more nuanced than for the Redtop: going too large risks losing the Blacktop's prized low-to-mid-range torque character, while staying stock leaves untapped potential in the 51 mm system.
