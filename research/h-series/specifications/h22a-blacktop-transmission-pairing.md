## Summary

The Honda H22A Blacktop (200 PS, black valve cover) was paired with multiple transmission codes across its JDM and international applications from 1992–2001. The primary factory transmissions were the M2A4 (open diff, STD), M2B4 (viscous LSD, SiR S-Spec/ Sport Package R-2), M2Y4 (open diff, 5th gen Base), M2Z4 (viscous LSD, 5th gen SiR S-Spec), and the ATTS-exclusive M2U4. All H22A Blacktop transmissions share a 4.266:1 final drive ratio. The Accord SiR sedan/wagon (CD6/CF2) used F-series P2A5/P2U5 transmissions with a taller 4.062:1 final drive. Factory LSD was available on SiR S-Spec and Sport Package R-2 models via viscous coupling units; aftermarket options include Quaife ATB, MFactory helical, and Cusco mechanical lock-up.

## Limited-Slip Differential (LSD)

### Factory LSD Type: Viscous Coupling

All factory H-series transmissions with LSD use a **viscous coupling** limited-slip differential:

| Transmission | LSD Type | Locking Characteristic |
|--------------|----------|----------------------|
| M2B4 (4th Gen SiR LSD) | Viscous LSD | Progressive lock-up based on temperature/slip |
| M2T4 (5th Gen SiR LSD) | Viscous LSD | Same viscous type |
| M2Z4 (5th Gen SiR S-Spec LSD) | Viscous LSD | Same viscous type |
| M2U4 (Type-SH ATTS) | ATTS system | Electronically controlled clutch pack (NOT a traditional LSD) |

**Important:** Viscous LSD units provide progressive, temperature-dependent lockup. This is less aggressive than the helical LSD found in later Accord Euro-R (T2W4) and EDM Type-R (U2Q7) transmissions. For track use, many enthusiasts upgrade to a helical LSD or swap to a T2W4/T2T4 transmission.

## Transmission Identification

All H-series transmissions can be identified by the code stamped on the bellhousing or on a metal tag attached to the transmission case. The code follows the pattern **MXX#** where:

- **M** = Honda Prelude (M-prefix denotes Prelude transmissions)
- **XX** = Variant identifier (A, B, T, Y, Z, U, etc.)
- **#** = Generation/batch number (4, 5, etc.)

To identify an unknown transmission:
1. Look for a metal tag or stamped code on the bellhousing
2. Count gear teeth on input/output/countershafts (see Honda-Tech FAQ "How to determine tranny gear ratios without counting teeth")
3. Compare against the master list in this document

All H-series M-prefix transmissions are **fully interchangeable** with each other in terms of bellhousing pattern, clutch, flywheel, and driveshaft mounting. The only exception is ATTS transmissions (M2U4), which require removal of the ATTS mechanism to fit non-ATTS chassis.

## Common Issues

### 5th Gear Synchro Wear

A well-documented issue with all H-series transmissions is premature 5th gear synchro wear, particularly in the M2B4 and M2F4. Symptoms include grinding when shifting into 5th under load. Honda issued a Technical Service Bulletin (TSB) for this issue, but reports indicate the fix is often temporary. Affected components:

- 5th/R synchronizer sleeve assembly
- 5th gear blocker ring
- Shift linkage bushings (contributes to poor engagement)

### Oil Specification

H-series transmissions use **manual transmission oil** (not engine oil). Recommended specification:

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Viscosity | 75W-90 GL-4 |
| Capacity | ~2.1 liters (drain and refill) |
| OEM recommendation | Honda Manual Transmission Fluid (part 08200-9008) |
| Aftermarket equivalent | Motul 75W-90, Red Line MT-90, AMSOIL Synthetic Manual Transmission Fluid |

**Warning:** Do not use GL-5 oil in H-series transmissions — the sulfur-containing additives corrode the brass synchro rings. Only GL-4 or specifically rated synthetic manual transmission fluids should be used.

### Viscous LSD Maintenance

Viscous LSD units do not require regular fluid changes, but the housing should be inspected for leaks. If the LSD becomes sluggish (common after 15+ years), replacement with a helical LSD or new viscous unit is recommended.

## Notes

### Conflicts and Disputes

1. **M2B4 3rd gear ratio discrepancy:** One source (SuperHonda forum, cited in civicforums.com [4]) lists M2B4 3rd gear as 1.160, while all other sources (Honda-Tech master-list by Kronn and Bense, NZHondas service manual reference, PreludeOnline) list it as 1.360 — identical to the M2A4. The weight of evidence supports 1.360; the 1.160 figure may be a typo or confusion with another transmission. Additionally, flyrod (Honda-Tech member) physically disassembled an M2B4 and counted gear teeth, confirming 1.360 as correct ([10]).

2. **M2T4 gear ratios not published:** The Honda-Tech master-list by Kronn lists M2T4 with no gear ratios populated. Based on cross-referencing with Bense's supplementary data, the M2T4 shares the same 5th-gen short-ratio gear set as the M2Y4/M2Z4 (3.285 / 1.956 / 1.344 / 1.034 / 0.812) with a 4.266:1 final drive. This is consistent with its classification as a BB6 H22A SiR LSD unit.

3. **M2F4 5th gear rounding:** Some sources list M2F4 5th as 0.871 rather than 0.870. This is a rounding difference and does not affect practical performance.

4. **Accord SiR transmission code ambiguity:** The CD6/CF2 Accord SiR appears to have used F-series P2A5/P2U5 transmissions (4.062:1 final drive), though some sources suggest H-series M2C4 units may also appear. This requires further verification against Honda service bulletins.

5. **M2B4 vs M2F4 interchangeability:** While both share the same gear ratios (except 4th/5th), the M2F4 has taller 4th (1.071 vs 1.034) and 5th (0.870 vs 0.787) gears for highway cruising. The M2B4 LSD unit can be fitted into an M2F4 case if the LSD internals are swapped.

### Key Takeaways for Blacktop Owners

- **4th gen SiR (1992–1996):** Most desirable pairing is M2B4 with viscous LSD — short 1st gear (3.307) for acceleration, tight 2nd–4th for track use, 4.266:1 final drive for responsive power delivery.
- **5th gen SiR (1997–2001):** M2T4 (SiR LSD) or M2Z4 (S-Spec LSD) with short-ratio 5th-gen gearing (3.285 1st) provides better highway cruising than 4th-gen units.
- **5th gen Base (USDM):** M2Y4 open diff is the stock pairing; LSD conversion via Traction Concepts kit ([11]) or Quaife drop-in is a common upgrade.
- **All M-prefix units** are interchangeable with each other (bellhousing, clutch, flywheel, driveshaft). ATTS units (M2U4) require modification.
- **Final drive upgrades** from MFactory (4.64:1) or Prodrive (4.71:1) are available for all H-series transmissions.
