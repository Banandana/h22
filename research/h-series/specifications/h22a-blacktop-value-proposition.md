## Summary

Comprehensive analysis of the H22A Blacktop's value proposition across three dimensions: used vehicle/engine pricing, total cost of ownership, and modification investment returns. The Blacktop represents the best bang-for-buck entry point into the H-series performance ecosystem — lower compression (10.6:1 vs Redtop's 11.0:1) provides more ignition advance margin and fuel flexibility, making it slightly more boost-tolerant and more forgiving of poor fuel quality. Engine swap kits range $2,249–$4,995 depending on OBD version and mileage; complete 4th gen Prelude Si vehicles with Blacktop-equivalent H22A1 engines range $4,800–$45,000+ depending on condition, mileage, and collector status. NA bolt-on Stage 1 modifications typically yield 15–20 HP ($300–$800 investment → 2–7% ROI per HP), while full turbo builds can achieve 300–360 HP at $5,000–$12,000 total investment. The Blacktop's lower CR gives it ~3–5° more timing advance margin than the Redtop, translating to slightly higher boost tolerance (~6–8 psi stock bottom end vs Redtop's ~5–7 psi).

### Value Comparison: Blacktop vs Redtop Modification Potential

| Parameter | H22A Blacktop | H22A Redtop | Advantage |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|-----------|
| Base power | 200 PS @ 7,000 RPM | 220 PS @ 7,200 RPM | Redtop (+20 PS) |
| Compression | 10.6:1 | 11.0:1 | Blacktop (more timing margin) |
| Ignition advance margin | +3–5° vs Redtop | Baseline | Blacktop |
| Boost tolerance (stock bottom) | ~6–8 psi | ~5–7 psi | Blacktop (slightly) |
| Fuel flexibility | 91 RON OK | Requires 98 RON premium | Blacktop |
| Factory head porting | None (as-cast) | Light CNC-style porting | Redtop (better starting point) |
| Head porting potential | Higher delta from as-cast | Lower delta from already-ported | Blacktop (more room for improvement) |
| Cam upgrade ROI | Better (lower base cams) | Good (higher base cams) | Blacktop |
| ECU tuning safety margin | Higher (less knock risk) | Lower (closer to knock limit) | Blacktop |
| **Best use case** | Budget turbo build, daily driver, fuel-flexible | Track-focused NA, high-RPM character | Different strengths |

**Key insight:** The Blacktop's lower compression ratio (10.6:1 vs 11.0:1) is its modification superpower. It provides ~3–5° more ignition advance before knock, which translates to:
- More aggressive cam profiles without knock risk
- Higher boost tolerance on turbo builds (~6–8 psi vs ~5–7 psi)
- Better tolerance of lower-octane fuel (91 RON vs required 98 RON)
- More forgiving ECU tuning for beginners

The trade-off is that the Redtop starts with ~20 PS more peak power and light factory head porting. For pure NA builds targeting 220–240 HP, the Redtop may require less work. For turbo builds or budget-conscious projects, the Blacktop offers better modification ROI.

## Cost of Ownership Risk Factors

### Primary Risk: FRM Liner Degradation

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **What it is** | Fiber-Reinforced Metal cylinder liner coating wears over time, causing oil consumption and compression loss |
| **Symptoms** | Blue smoke on startup, oil level dropping 1 quart per 1,000 km, compression test variance >10% between cylinders |
| **Prevention** | Frequent oil changes (3K–5K miles), oil catch can, PCV system maintenance, synthetic oil (5W-30 or 5W-40) |
| **Repair: Sleeving** | RS Machine sleeves: $800–$1,500 parts, requires machine shop, total $2,500–$4,000 with labor |
| **Repair: Replacement** | Remanufactured short block: $2,000–$3,500; new custom block: $5,000+ |
| **Likelihood** | High after 150K miles or poor maintenance; moderate with good maintenance |

## Notes

### Conflicts and Disputes

1. **Blacktop power figures vary by source:** Some sources list 200 PS @ 7,000 RPM, others 197 hp (SAE net). Working assumption: 200 PS (DIN) = 197 hp (SAE net) @ 7,000 RPM. Accord SiR variant rated lower at 190 PS due to different calibration.

2. **Used car prices have extreme variance:** The Classic.com data shows a 958× spread between lowest ($4,800) and highest ($45,000) recorded sale. This reflects the difference between project cars and collector-grade examples. Median is more representative: ~$12,000–$15,000 for drivable examples.

3. **Engine pricing fluctuates with JDM supply:** eBay listings show wide variance ($2,695–$4,995) based on mileage, OBD version, and whether transmission/ECU are included. Prices tend to rise as donor vehicles become scarce.

4. **Modification gain estimates are conservative:** TorqueCars and community sources suggest 15% NA gains are achievable, but real-world results depend on baseline engine condition. A poorly maintained Blacktop may gain less from bolt-ons than a fresh one.

5. **FRM liner timeline is uncertain:** No definitive mileage threshold for sleeve failure exists. Symptoms appear variably between 100K–200K+ miles depending on maintenance. This makes lifetime cost estimation difficult.

6. **Collector appreciation is unpredictable:** While low-mile 4th gen Preldues have appreciated significantly (998-mile example $40K), this trend may not continue linearly. The market is small and sentiment-driven.

### Identification Tips Relevant to Value

- **Verify engine is genuine Blacktop:** Check throttle body size (60 mm = Blacktop, 62.5 mm = Redtop), exhaust diameter (51 mm = Blacktop, 57 mm = Redtop), and cam duration if possible
- **Valve cover color is unreliable:** Covers are frequently repainted; physical measurements are more reliable
- **Block serial code determines variant:** See h22a-blacktop-jdm-origin.md for serial code ranges
- **OBD version affects value:** OBD1 engines (pre-1997) are easier to tune (socketed chip) and often preferred by tuners; OBD2 (1997+) have immobilizer complications
