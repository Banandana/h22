## Summary

The Honda H22A Redtop (220 PS / 217 hp JDM) delivers strong performance for a 1990s front-wheel-drive sports coupe. The 5th-gen SiR S Spec (BB6/BB8) with 220 PS achieves an estimated 0–60 mph in 6.4 seconds and 0–100 km/h in 6.7 seconds, while the 4th-gen Si VTEC (USDM H22A1, 190–197 hp) clocks 0–60 mph in 6.9–7.0 seconds. Quarter-mile times range from 14.6 seconds (Si VTEC) to 15.5 seconds (Type SH). Both 4th and 5th-gen Preludes recorded identical Tsukuba Circuit lap times of ~1:12.0, placing them among the fastest FWD Hondas of their era. No Nürburgring Nordschleife lap times were found in any consulted source — the Prelude was never officially tested there during its production run.

## Nürburgring Nordschleife — No Data Found

Despite extensive searching, **no Nürburgring Nordschleife lap times were found for any generation of Honda Prelude** in any consulted source. This is consistent with the Prelude's market positioning — it was never an official race car at the 'Ring like the NSX or Integra Type R, and no magazine tests or owner records appear online. A Reddit thread (r/hondaprelude, 2024) discusses a Prelude at the Nürburgring but with an F20 swap, confirming that H22-equipped Preludes are rare at the track in Europe.

This represents a significant gap in the performance data corpus. If Nürburgring times exist, they would likely come from:
- Japanese owner-run track day recordings (not indexed online)
- Best Motoring or Option magazine tests (archived print media)
- Private owner telemetry data

## Conflicts and Ambiguities

1. **JDM SiR S Spec weight:** FastestLaps lists 1,270 kg; Carfolio lists 1,310 kg. This 40 kg discrepancy affects power-to-weight calculations and acceleration estimates. The lower figure (1,270 kg) may represent an empty-fuel weight or exclude driver weight.

2. **JDM SiR S Spec 0–60 mph:** The 6.4-second estimate from automobile-catalog.com (ProfessCars algorithm) is theoretical, not measured. No direct 0–60 test was found for the JDM SiR S Spec in English-language sources. Japanese magazine tests (Best Motoring, Option, Car Graphic) may contain measured data but are not accessible in English.

3. **Best Motoring test car identity:** The Best Motoring 1994 2L N/A Tsukuba Battle test shows a 1:12.03 lap and 14.6-second quarter mile. FastestLaps attributes this to the "Prelude Si VTEC (Mk IV)" — i.e., the 4th-gen USDM/H22A1. However, the 1994 model year could also include early JDM SiR S-Spec (Type S) models. The distinction matters because the 220 PS Redtop would be expected to outperform the 190–197 hp USDM Si VTEC.

4. **MotorWeek 6.3-second 0–60:** One Instagram clip claims a 6.3-second 0–60 for a 1993 Prelude VTEC. This is an outlier compared to the 6.9–7.8 second range from other sources. Likely represents ideal conditions (cold tires, downhill run, pre-production prototype) rather than a representative production-car result.

5. **Type SH braking distance:** C/D's 1999 retro test recorded 195 ft from 70–0 mph, which was "significantly longer than what we've previously recorded for the Type SH." This suggests brake fade from aging pads/rotors on the low-mileage museum car rather than a genuine performance deficit.

## Notes

### Key Observations

1. **The 4th-gen Si VTEC and 5th-gen SiR S Spec achieved virtually identical Tsukuba lap times** (1:12.03 vs 1:12.13), despite the 5th-gen having 20+ more horsepower. This suggests the 5th-gen's additional weight (larger chassis, ATTS system in Type SH) offset its power advantage. The 0.1-second difference is within measurement variance.

2. **The H22A Redtop's quarter-mile time of 14.6 seconds** (Best Motoring) is impressive for a stock FWD car in the mid-1990s. For comparison, the contemporary Nissan Silvia S13 (185 hp, RWD) typically ran 15.0–15.5 seconds, and the Mazda RX-7 FD (255 hp, RWD) ran ~14.0 seconds. The Redtop's lightweight (~1,230–1,310 kg) and advanced traction management (ATTS in Type SH) contributed to its strong launch performance.

3. **No Nürburgring data exists** for any Prelude generation. This is a notable gap, especially given the Prelude's reputation as a handling masterpiece. The absence likely reflects the car's lack of official motorsport involvement at the 'Ring — unlike the NSX (which raced in Super GT) or Integra Type R (which appeared in touring car championships).

4. **The EDM H22A2 (185 PS) at 7.3 sec 0–100 km/h** is roughly 0.4 seconds slower than the JDM Redtop's estimated 6.9 sec, reflecting the 35 PS detuning for European emissions compliance. This is a reasonable gap for a 15% power reduction.

5. **The 5th-gen Type SH's Car and Driver test (200 hp, 7.0 sec 0–60)** is the most reliable American-market performance figure for an H22-prelude. The test car had only 941 miles and was from Honda's own collection, making it essentially new-old-stock.

### Gaps Identified

- **JDM Redtop 0–60 / 0–100 km/h not directly measured.** All figures are algorithmic estimates from automobile-catalog.com. Japanese magazine tests (Best Motoring, Option, Car Graphic) may contain measured data but are not accessible in English.
- **Quarter-mile time for 5th-gen SiR S Spec / Type S not published.** Only Tsukuba lap time is available.
- **Nürburgring Nordschleife lap times: none found.** Significant gap requiring access to Japanese-language track day records or archived magazine tests.
- **Top speed figures not published** for JDM variants (speed limiter at 180 km/h / 112 mph by Japanese voluntary agreement).
- **Braking distances** not available for JDM variants; only 5th-gen Type SH has published 70–0 mph braking (195 ft, from aging brakes).
- **Skidpad / lateral grip figures** not available for JDM variants; only 5th-gen Type SH has published data (0.88 g from C/D).
