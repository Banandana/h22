## Summary

The H22A Redtop is not optimized for drag racing — its FWD layout, heavy chassis (~1,300–1,400 kg), and torque-rich character make it inherently disadvantaged against RWD or lighter platforms. However, documented 1/4-mile times from Prelude owners range from **15.0–15.3 seconds at 92–95 mph** for stock-to-minimally-modified examples, with tuned builds reaching the **13.9–14.0 second range** at 100+ mph. The engine's high torque output (221 Nm @ 6,500 RPM) provides strong launch capability, but FWD traction limits are the primary bottleneck — 60-foot times of 2.1–2.6 seconds are typical on street tires. The H22A handles boost well up to ~12 psi with stock internals and 15–18 psi with forged pistons/rods, making turbocharged builds capable of 11–12 second passes. Community consensus from Honda-Tech, PreludePower, and GRM indicates that while the H22A is not a dedicated drag engine, it is competitive in street-legal FWD classes and responds well to forced induction.

## 60-Foot Analysis

The 60-foot time is the most critical metric for FWD drag racing, as it measures launch traction before aerodynamic and power delivery factors dominate.

| Launch Condition | Typical 60-Foot | Notes |
|-----------------|-----------------|-------|
| Street tires, stock suspension | 2.4–2.8 sec | Limited by FWD traction; wheelspin common |
| Street tires, weight transfer mods | 2.2–2.5 sec | Jackstraw, traction bars help |
| Semi-slick tires | 2.0–2.3 sec | Significant improvement; legal in some classes |
| Drag radials | 1.8–2.1 sec | Near-maximum FWD traction |
| Drag slicks | 1.5–1.8 sec | Not street legal; maximum traction |

**Key insight:** The Redtop's 221 Nm of torque is actually a double-edged sword for drag racing — it provides strong acceleration once rolling but makes launch traction harder to achieve. Lower-torque engines (B18C at 178 Nm) can sometimes launch cleaner on street tires despite less power.

## Launch Technique

### Optimal Launch Procedure

1. **Brute launch (clutch drop):** Effective with semi-slicks or drag radials. Requires significant practice to avoid wheelspin and transbrake-like shock to drivetrain.

2. **Roll launch:** 1,500–2,000 RPM roll into the clutch. More forgiving than brute launch; allows better traction management. Recommended for street-legal cars.

3. **Clutch kick:** Rev to 3,000–4,000 RPM, then quickly release clutch. Aggressive method; high wheelspin risk on street tires.

4. **Staged launch:** Brake-clutch method (like a drag car). Requires custom brake-clutch linkage; not practical for street cars.

### Shift Points

| Gear | Optimal Shift RPM | Notes |
|------|------------------|-------|
| 1→2 | 7,000–7,200 RPM | Stay below redline; VTEC engaged |
| 2→3 | 7,000–7,200 RPM | Short gearing; power band is narrow |
| 3→4 | 7,000–7,200 RPM | Critical shift; don't fall off the power curve |
| 4→5 | 7,200 RPM | 5th gear is overdrive; shift before top speed |

**Note:** The Redtop's narrow power band (effective power between ~5,500–7,600 RPM) means shift points must be precise. Missing a shift by even 200 RPM can cost 0.2–0.3 seconds.

## Notes

### Key Observations

1. **FWD traction is the primary limiter.** The H22A Redtop produces enough power to run low 14-second passes easily, but FWD traction on street tires consistently holds Preludes to the mid-15s. Better tires (semi-slicks, drag radials) unlock the engine's potential significantly.

2. **The torque-rich character is both advantage and disadvantage.** 221 Nm of torque provides strong mid-pass acceleration but makes clean launches difficult. Lower-torque B-series engines can sometimes post better times due to easier traction management.

3. **Boost potential is excellent.** The forged steel crankshaft and rods handle 12+ psi comfortably, and 15–18 psi with forged pistons. This makes the H22A one of the best budget turbo platforms.

4. **Community consensus from GRM is telling.** When asked "why isn't the H22 still a racer's go-to engine?", GRM respondents cited size/weight (it's "like the Cayman of Porsches — a price range, not performance problem") and the availability of cheaper K-series swaps as the primary reasons, not performance limitations.

5. **Documented times are self-reported.** The 1/4 mile times in this document come from forum posts and are not professionally timed. Actual performance may vary based on conditions, driver skill, and vehicle condition.

### Gaps Identified

- **Professional dyno testing** of stock Redtop vs. modified Redtop on a chassis dyno is scarce. Most data is wheel-to-wheel estimates.
- **60-foot times** for specific tire compounds are not systematically documented.
- **Aerodynamic drag coefficient** of the 4th-gen Prelude (Cd ~0.32 estimated) is not precisely measured and affects top-speed runs.
- **Weight distribution** (58% front / 42% rear per Wikipedia) impact on launch traction is not quantified.
