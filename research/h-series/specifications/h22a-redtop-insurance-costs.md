## Summary

Insurance costs for H22A Redtop-equipped vehicles (primarily JDM 4th/5th-generation Honda Preludes) vary significantly based on vehicle classification, market, driver profile, and coverage type. As of 2026, the 4th-gen Prelude (1992–1996) and 5th-gen Prelude (1997–2001) are **not yet classified as classic cars** under most insurer definitions (which typically require vehicles produced in 1979 or earlier), meaning they fall into the "collectible" or "vintage" category at best. Standard full-coverage insurance for a used 4th-gen Prelude ranges from **$800–1,500/year** depending on location, driver age, and coverage level. Classic/car collector insurance, once the vehicle qualifies (or through special collector programs), drops to **$200–600/year** for agreed-value policies. Key cost drivers include the vehicle's sport coupe classification, increasing used values ($6,000–15,000+ for clean Redtop examples), performance-oriented driver demographics, and limited parts availability in some regions. Honda vehicles generally have below-average insurance costs due to strong reliability records and low theft rates compared to other sports coupes.

## Notes

### Key Observations

1. **The Redtop Prelude is not yet a "classic" by traditional standards.** Most insurers define classic cars as 1979 or earlier. The 4th-gen Prelude (1992–1996) will reach the 30-year mark in 2022–2026, making it eligible for many collector car programs but not all traditional classic policies.

2. **Agreed value is essential for appreciating vehicles.** As Redtop Preludes continue to appreciate, an agreed-value policy ensures you receive the pre-negotiated amount in a total loss — not a depreciated actual cash value that may not cover replacement cost.

3. **Honda's reliability reputation helps.** Honda vehicles consistently rank below the national average for insurance costs. The H22A Redtop's strong reliability record (200,000+ mile lifespans documented) further reduces claim frequency.

4. **Modified vehicles face significant surcharges.** A turbocharged, chipped, or heavily modified Redtop Prelude may face 25–50% premium increases. Disclose modifications honestly; non-disclosure can void coverage.

5. **Classic car insurance requires usage restrictions.** Collector policies typically limit annual mileage (2,500–5,000 miles), require garaged storage, and restrict use to shows/exhibitions/occasional pleasure driving. Using a collector-policy vehicle as a daily driver violates terms and can void coverage.
