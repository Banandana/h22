## 5. Cylinder Liner / Sleeving Solutions

The H22A's FRM (Fiber-Reinforced Metal) cylinder liners present a unique challenge for rebuilds. FRM cannot be machined conventionally and requires special honing procedures (diamond/BNB stones). For high-power builds or engines with worn liners, aftermarket sleeve solutions are necessary:

| Solution | Type | Notes |
|----------|------|-------|
| **RS Machine Sleeves** | Ductile iron | Most common aftermarket solution; requires full block resleeving |
| **Darton Dry Sleeves** | Steel dry sleeves | 300-018 (standard bore 87mm), 400-160-P (400 series, 87–90mm) |
| **LA Sleeve Co.** | Ductile iron sleeves (LA 38) | Reported cost ~$100 for sleeves only; installation + bore + hone ~$510 at machine shop |

**FRM Considerations:**
- OEM FRM liners are difficult to machine and require diamond or boron nitride honing stones
- Accelerated piston ring wear can occur due to FRM hardness (~9 on Mohs scale)
- For boosted/high-RPM builds, sleeving is strongly recommended
- Cannot be machined to oversize like traditional cast iron liners — full resleeving required for rebore

## 6. Bolt Kit Availability

Complete bolt kits for H22A rebuilds are less commonly packaged than gasket kits. Builders typically source bolts individually:

**Critical Fasteners to Replace/Upgrade:**
- **Head bolts:** Torque-to-yield (TTY) — replace with ARP studs (ARP #208-4304, $214)
- **Main bearing cap bolts:** TTY — upgrade to ARP mains (ARP #208-5401, $144)
- **Rod bolts:** Replace with ARP2000 if building for performance (ARP #4AJ1.600-2SU, $89 each)
- **Timing belt tensioner bolt:** 33 ft-lb torque spec
- **Crank pulley bolt:** 245 N·m / 181 lb-ft torque spec
- **Cam pulley bolts:** 43 ft-lb torque spec
- **Oil pan bolts:** 7 ft-lb torque spec (aluminum pan, over-torquing causes leaks)
- **Head bolt torque sequence:** Multi-stage (typically 3-stage: initial torque + angle turns)

**Users seeking complete bolt kits:**
- Facebook group discussion (2026) indicates demand for pre-packaged H22A bolt kits
- No widely available commercial "complete bolt kit" found in current research
- Most builders source ARP upgrade kits and replace consumable bolts (head bolts, crush washers) individually

## 10. Notes and Caveats

1. **Price volatility:** All cost figures are approximate and based on available data as of 2026-05-15. Prices for used engines, parts, and aftermarket components fluctuate with market demand. H22A values have been appreciating as 1990s VTEC icons gain collector status.

2. **OBD1 vs OBD2 differences:** Part numbers and dimensions differ between OBD1 (1992–1996, H22A1/H22A2) and OBD2 (1997–2001, H22A4) variants. Main journal diameter changed from 50mm to 55mm in 1998+. Always verify compatibility before purchasing.

3. **Labor cost variability:** Shop labor rates vary significantly by region and shop specialization. Shops that regularly work on Hondas/H-series will be faster and potentially cheaper than generalist shops.

4. **FRM limitations:** The FRM cylinder liner coating cannot be machined to oversize. For engines requiring rebore, aftermarket sleeve installation (RS Machine, Darton, LA Sleeve Co.) is required. This adds $500–$1,100 to rebuild costs (sleeves + machine shop labor).

5. **No complete bolt kit commercially available:** Research did not find a widely available pre-packaged "complete H22A bolt kit." Most builders source ARP upgrade kits and replace consumable bolts individually.

6. **Community knowledge gap:** While extensive documentation exists for individual components and procedures, there is no single comprehensive "H22A restoration guide" published as a book or official manual. Most procedural knowledge comes from community forums, YouTube videos, and scattered service manual excerpts.
