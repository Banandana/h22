## Summary

This document compiles all known weight and balance data for vehicles equipped with the Honda H22A Redtop engine (220 PS, 11.0:1 CR). The H22A Redtop appeared in four JDM vehicle applications: the 4th-gen Prelude SiR S-Spec (BB1/BB4, 1992–1996), the 5th-gen Prelude Type S (BB6, 1997–2001), the Accord SiR sedan/wagon (CD6/CF2, 1994–1997), and the Accord Euro-R / Torneo Euro-R (CL1, 1999–2001). Curb weights range from approximately 1,290 kg (SiR S-Spec) to 1,390 kg (Torneo Euro-R). All applications are front-heavy with weight distribution ranging from 58/42 to 63/37 front/rear. Center of gravity height is not documented by any accessible source — this remains an open gap.

## Notes

### Key Observations

1. **Front-heavy across all applications.** Every H22A Redtop application exhibits front-biased weight distribution, ranging from 58/42 (4th-gen SiR S-Spec) to 63/37 (5th-gen Type S). This is typical for front-engine FWD sports cars but contributes to understeer character, which the ATTS system was specifically designed to mitigate.

2. **5th-gen is heavier than 4th-gen.** The 5th-gen Type S (1,310 kg) is approximately 20–40 kg heavier than the 4th-gen SiR S-Spec (~1,290 kg), despite both using the same engine. This reflects the larger body, additional safety equipment, and the ATTS system on the Type S.

3. **ATTS adds measurable weight.** The ATTS MCU adds ~40 lbs (18 kg) to the Type SH variant. This weight is concentrated low and near the vehicle's center of gravity (between the differential and driveshafts), minimizing its impact on handling balance.

4. **Center of gravity height is undocumented.** Despite extensive searching across Honda press releases, enthusiast forums, vehicle databases, and automotive publications, no source provides the center of gravity height for any H22A Redtop application. This is a notable gap in the public record. The low roll center (58 mm) and low vehicle height (1,315 mm) suggest a low CG, but no verified number exists.

5. **Accord Euro-R and Torneo share the CL1 platform.** The sedan (1,330 kg) and formal sedan (1,390 kg) differ by 60 kg, likely due to the Torneo's additional sound insulation, heavier body panels, and more luxurious trim. Both share the same H22A Redtop engine and weight distribution characteristics.

6. **Engine is ~13–14% of total vehicle weight.** The H22A Redtop long block at ~175 kg represents a consistent fraction of total vehicle weight across all applications, indicating Honda's focus on power density and lightweight packaging.

### Conflicts and Ambiguities

1. **4th-gen SiR S-Spec weight distribution:** Estimated at 58/42 based on comparison with the 5th-gen (63/37) and the 4th-gen's lower hoodline. No factory documentation confirms this figure. The 4th-gen's lighter body and lower CG should reduce front bias compared to the 5th-gen.

2. **5th-gen weight distribution variance:** Honda Global press release states 63/37, while a preludeonline.com forum thread (Aug 2003) records approximately 62/38 from owner experience. The 1% difference may reflect test conditions (fuel level, driver weight, spare tire presence). Factory spec is 63/37.

3. **Accord SiR (CD6/CF2) weight:** Not directly sourced. Estimated from platform relationship to the Prelude (same H22A engine, similar suspension geometry) plus sedan body penalty (~50–100 kg over equivalent coupe).

4. **Center of gravity height:** No source found. This is not a conflict — it is a confirmed gap. The closest available metric is the front roll center height (58 mm), which is a suspension geometry parameter, not the vehicle CG.
