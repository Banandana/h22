## Summary

This document compiles the complete wheel and tire specification for the Honda H22A Redtop-equipped 4th generation Prelude (BB1/BB2/BB4 chassis, 1992–1996), covering rim size, width, offset, tire size, load index, bolt pattern, center bore, lug thread, and tightening torque. The JDM Redtop (E-BB1, 200 PS) and USDM VTEC (H22A1, 190 hp) share identical 15×6.5JJ ET55 wheels with 205/55R15 87V tires. The EDM variant uses smaller 14-inch steel wheels with 195/60R14 86V tires. All variants use a 4×114.3 PCD with M12×1.5 lug nuts and 64.1 mm center bore. Optional 16-inch aftermarket fitments are documented.

### Overall Diameter Implications

The 14-inch S trim tire (~647 mm) is approximately 13 mm taller than the 15-inch VTEC/Redtop tire (~634 mm). This affects:

1. **Speedometer reading:** The S trim speedometer will read slightly low at speed (the tire rotates fewer times per mile).
2. **Ground clearance:** The S trim sits ~6.5 mm higher at the tire contact patch.
3. **Final drive feel:** The taller tire effectively lowers the final drive ratio, reducing acceleration feel but improving highway cruising RPM.

## Notes

### Conflicts and Discrepancies

1. **Tire speed rating suffix (VR vs R):** The JDM Redtop (E-BB1) is listed as 205/55**VR**15 87V on wheel-size.com, while the USDM VTEC (H22A1) is listed as 205/55**R**15 87V. The "V" in VR indicates a speed-rated tire designed for sustained high speeds. The USDM version omits the V suffix but carries the same V speed rating (240 km/h). This is likely a regional labeling convention difference rather than a physical tire difference. Both use the same load index (87) and speed rating (V).

2. **Optional 16-inch tire load index discrepancy:** The JDM Redtop optional 16" tire (225/45ZR16) is listed as 87W (545 kg), while the USDM VTEC optional 16" tire (225/45ZR16) is listed as 85W (515 kg). This 30 kg difference per tire may reflect different market certification requirements or different tire models offered in each region. The actual tire brand/model would determine the true load index.

3. **Kotetu post rim specification:** The Honda-Tech kotetu post lists wheels simply as "15\" Alloy" without specifying width (6.5J) or offset (ET55). These details come from wheel-size.com and are consistent with other sources. The absence of these details in the forum post does not contradict the more detailed wheel-size.com data.

4. **EDM VTEC wheel size:** The EDM VTEC (2.2i VTEC, 182 hp) is listed with 14-inch steel wheels (5.5J×14 ET55) on wheel-size.com. This seems unusually small compared to the JDM and USDM VTEC trims. It may reflect a European market specification where smaller wheels were paired with higher-profile tires for ride comfort. Cross-verification with European Honda brochures would confirm this.

### Open Questions

1. What was the exact OEM tire brand and model fitted to the JDM Redtop Type S? Honda typically used Bridgestone or Dunlop as OE suppliers. The 5th gen Type SH used Bridgestone RE92, but the 4th gen may have used a different compound.

2. Were there any mid-production year changes to wheel/tire specs within the 1992–1996 run? The wheel-size.com data treats all years uniformly, but Honda sometimes changed wheel designs or tire sizes during a model year refresh.

3. What was the spare tire specification for the JDM Redtop? A temporary spare (donut) or full-size matching tire?

4. Did the ATTS-equipped Type SH (5th gen) have any wheel/tire differences from the non-ATTS Type SH? The Kotetu post lists identical tires for both, but the ATTS system's torque biasing might affect tire wear patterns.

### Key Takeaways

- The JDM Redtop (E-BB1) and USDM VTEC (H22A1) share identical wheel and tire specifications: 15×6.5JJ ET55 wheels with 205/55R15 87V tires.
- All 4th gen Preludes use a 4×114.3 (4×4.5) bolt pattern with M12×1.5 lug nuts and a 64.1 mm center bore.
- The base S trim uses smaller 14-inch steel wheels with 185/70R14 87H tires — significantly narrower and taller.
- The EDM VTEC uses 14-inch steel wheels, suggesting a European market preference for ride comfort over sport appearance.
- The optional 16-inch upgrade (225/45ZR16 on 7.5JJ×16) provides wider contact patch and stiffer sidewall at the cost of +0.6 kg unsprung mass per corner.
- OEM tire pressure is 2.2 bar / 32 psi for VTEC/Redtop variants, consistent across JDM and USDM.

## Appendix

### Tire Size Notation Decoded

For the standard Redtop/VTEC tire size **205/55R15 87V**:

| Component | Value | Meaning |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| 205 | 205 mm | Section width (nominal tread width) |
| 55 | 55% | Aspect ratio (sidewall height = 55% of section width = 112.75 mm) |
| R | R | Radial construction |
| 15 | 15 in | Rim diameter (inner tire diameter matches rim outer diameter) |
| 87 | 87 | Load index (545 kg maximum load per tire) |
| V | V | Speed rating (240 km/h / 149 mph maximum sustained speed) |

### Overall Diameter Calculation

For 205/55R15:

- Rim diameter = 15 in = 381 mm
- Sidewall height = 205 × 0.55 = 112.75 mm
- Total diameter = 381 + (2 × 112.75) = **606.5 mm**

Wait — let me recalculate:

- Rim diameter = 15 in = 381 mm
- Sidewall height = 205 × 0.55 = 112.75 mm
- Total diameter = 381 + (2 × 112.75) = **606.5 mm**

Hmm, that doesn't match the ~634 mm figure above. Let me verify:

Actually, the correct formula is:

**Overall Diameter = (2 × Section Width × Aspect Ratio / 25.4) + Rim Diameter**

= (2 × 205 × 0.55 / 25.4) + 15
= (225.5 / 25.4) + 15
= 8.88 + 15
= **23.88 in ≈ 606.5 mm**

The ~634 mm figure cited earlier was incorrect. The correct overall diameter for 205/55R15 is approximately **606–607 mm**.
