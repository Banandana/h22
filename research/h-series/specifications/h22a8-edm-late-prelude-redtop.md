## Summary

The H22A8 is the final evolution of the European-market H-series engine, produced from 1999 to 2001 and fitted exclusively in the 5th-generation Honda Prelude 2.2VTi and VTi-S (BB6/BB8 chassis). Officially rated at 200 PS (147 kW; 197 hp) at 7,100 RPM with 212 Nm of torque at 5,250 RPM, it replaced the earlier H22A5 and represented Honda's last hurrah for the H-series before the Prelude's discontinuation. Its most distinctive visual feature — a red valve cover — was unusual for a non-JDM engine and initially raised questions among enthusiasts about its provenance. However, community consensus and dyno evidence confirm that the H22A8 shares the same high-compression architecture as the JDM Type-S H22A (same cams, same 10.6–11.0:1 compression ratio), albeit with slightly different intake/exhaust manifolds tuned for European emissions compliance. This document compiles all available technical data, variant comparisons, market context, and community insights.

## The Red Valve Cover Anomaly

### Why It Matters

The H22A8's red valve cover was the subject of significant community discussion because:

1. **Non-JDM engines typically had black valve covers.** The H22A1 (USDM), H22A2 (EDM 4th gen), and H22A4 (USDM 5th gen) all used black covers. A red cover was associated almost exclusively with JDM performance models (H22A Redtop, H22A Type-S).

2. **The red cover signaled higher performance.** In Honda's color-coding system, red = high-compression, high-lift cam profile, "red top" performance variant. The H22A8 inherited this designation from the JDM Type-S it was based on.

3. **It caused confusion among owners and buyers.** Forum members frequently reported difficulty finding H22A8-equipped cars in Western Europe, with some Honda dealers dismissing the H22A8+VTi-S combination as a "hoax." Anabasis (PreludePower member) documented spending "much time looking for a VTi-S+H22A8" across multiple countries, with all local Honda HQs saying it didn't exist.

### Resolution

Community consensus (PreludePower, Honda-Tech, Wikipedia) confirms:

- **Red tops were indeed on the EUDM H22A8 and H22A7** (YellaLude, PreludePower, Feb 2007).
- **The H22A8 shares the same cams and compression ratio as the JDM Type-S H22A** (Infinity[77rus], PreludePower, Feb 2007).
- **Honda used the red cover to distinguish its final, highest-output H-series variant** — alongside the H22A7 (Accord Type R) and H22Z1 (Australian VTi-R ATTS, red cover on ATTS models).
- **As one forum member summarized:** "It makes sense that Honda's very last three editions of the H22 engine with the higher power and the higher compression ratio all had the same coloured — red — rocker cover as they did, namely the H22A7, H22A8 and the higher powered H22Z1" (CivicForums, H-series specs thread).

### Valve Cover Physical Differences

The red valve cover (used on H22A8, H22A7, JDM Type-S) differs physically from the blue valve cover (used on H23A VTEC, F20B):

- **Red top:** Standard alternator wire harness cover screw location; no extra tab on valve cover.
- **Blue top:** Has an extra tab on the valve cover with a threaded hole for relocated alternator wire harness cover (edge2lyfe, PreludePower, Sep 2007).

The H22A8 uses the red-top valve cover design, consistent with its JDM Type-S-derived architecture.

## Late-Production Characteristics

### Context: The 1999 Mid-Cycle Refresh

The H22A8 was introduced as part of the 5th-generation Prelude's mid-cycle refresh for the 1999 model year. According to Honda News (2001 specifications release), the refresh included:

- **5 hp increase** in power for manual transmission models (200 hp from 195 hp)
- **New front grille** featuring a small "Prelude" badge
- **Cabin air filtration system** access door for filter replacement without modifications
- **Revised color options**

The H22A8 was the mechanical heart of this refresh, upgrading the European-spec Prelude to match the power levels of the contemporary USDM H22A4 (which also received a 5 hp bump from 195 to 200 hp during the same refresh period).

### Production Timeline

| Period | Engine | Power | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|-------|
| 1997–1998 | H22A5 | 185 PS | Initial 5th-gen European spec |
| 1999–2001 | H22A8 | 200 PS | Mid-cycle refresh upgrade |
| End of production | — | — | Prelude discontinued 2001 |

### Rarity

The H22A8 was relatively rare even among 5th-gen Preludes:

- **Limited to 2WS models only** (no ATTS versions)
- **Introduced late in the 5th-gen lifecycle** (1999 vs 1997 start date)
- **Western European scarcity:** Anabasis reported that all Honda HQs in Italy, France, Spain, Germany, and the Netherlands said the H22A8+VTi-S combination was a "hoax" — suggesting it was extremely uncommon in LHD markets
- **RHD market prevalence:** Confirmed examples exist in UK (YellaLude's '99 Motegi VTi 4WS BB8), Australia (though Australia used H22Z1 for VTi-R ATTS), and Russia (Infinity[77rus]'s dyno test)
- **Community perception:** Considered one of the rarest European-market H22 variants, alongside the H22A7 (Accord Type R)

## Notes

### Conflicts & Uncertainties

1. **Compression ratio range (10.6:1 vs 11.0:1):** CivicForums lists "10.6-11.0:1" without specifying which year got which ratio. Honda-Tech (Chief_Rocka) states 11.0:1. No definitive source resolves this — it may vary by production batch or may reflect different measurement methodologies.

2. **Actual power output vs official rating:** The CivicForums thread notes that the H22A8 produces "200 PS (147 kW; 197 hp)" but also states that the H22Z1 (Australian equivalent) has disputed power ranging from 197 to 212 HP. The dyno test by Infinity[77rus] showed 198 whp for both H22A8 and JDM Type-S, which is lower than the claimed 200 PS (197 hp) for the H22A8 — likely due to drivetrain loss and measurement methodology.

3. **ATTS compatibility debate:** Anabasis reported that all Honda HQs in Western Europe said the H22A8+VTi-S+ATTS combination didn't exist, yet YellaLude (a well-known UK enthusiast) owned a '99 Prelude BB8 VTi 4WS Motegi with an H22A8. This suggests the H22A8 was sometimes retrofitted or swapped into ATTS cars, or that early 1999 models may have had transitional specification.

4. **Rarity in LHD markets:** Multiple sources suggest the H22A8 was extremely rare in continental Europe, with Honda dealers dismissing its existence. However, confirmed examples exist (Russia, UK). This may reflect regional distribution patterns rather than non-existence.

### Key Takeaways

- The H22A8 was Honda's final European H-series engine, produced 1999–2001 in the Prelude 2.2VTi/VTi-S (BB6/BB8).
- It gained 15 PS over the H22A5 primarily through increased compression (10.0:1 → 10.6–11.0:1), not new cam profiles.
- The red valve cover was intentional and signified its JDM Type-S-derived high-compression architecture — not a swap or anomaly.
- The H22A8 was NOT compatible with ATTS; it was exclusive to 2WS models.
- Community consensus confirms it shares the same cams and compression ratio as the JDM Type-S H22A, with different intake/exhaust manifolds tuned for European emissions.
- It was relatively rare, especially in LHD Western European markets where many Honda dealers reportedly denied its existence.

### Implications for Building/Modding

- **Swap compatibility:** The H22A8 head/block is directly compatible with other 5th-gen H22 blocks (H22A4, H22A5) since they share the same open-deck architecture. The higher compression ratio requires premium fuel (95+ RON).
- **Valve cover interchangeability:** Red valve covers from H22A8, H22A7, and JDM Type-S are interchangeable. Blue valve covers (H23A, F20B) are NOT directly interchangeable due to the alternator wire harness tab difference.
- **Aftermarket support:** As a late-production variant, the H22A8 benefits from the full aftermarket support ecosystem built around the H22A4/H22A5 platform. No unique parts requirements beyond standard H22 consumables.
- **Tuning potential:** The higher compression ratio (10.6–11.0:1) limits safe boost levels compared to the 10.0:1 H22A5 — stock bottom end should be considered for anything above ~6–7 psi.
