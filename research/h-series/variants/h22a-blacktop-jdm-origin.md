## Summary

The H22A Blacktop is the standard/base variant of Honda's H22A DOHC VTEC engine family, distinguished by its black valve cover. It was introduced in September 1991 alongside the H22A Redtop as part of the fourth-generation Honda Prelude (BB1 chassis) launch — the same event that debuted the entire H-series engine family. The Blacktop had a longer production run than the Redtop, spanning 1991–2001 across both the 4th gen (1992–1996, closed-deck) and 5th gen (1997–2001, open-deck) Preludes, as well as the Accord SiR (CD6/CF2) and Torneo Euro-R (CL1). It was JDM-exclusive at 200 PS; other markets received detuned variants (H22A1 USDM, H22A2 EDM, H22Z1 AUS) that were mechanically similar Blacktops with different calibrations and emissions equipment.

## Introduction and Origin

### September 1991 — H-Series Debut

The H22A Blacktop was introduced in **September 1991** as part of the fourth-generation Honda Prelude (BB1 chassis) launch. This was the same event that debuted the entire H-series engine family, which included two H22A variants:

1. **H22A Redtop** (220 PS, 11.0:1 CR, aggressive cams, light factory porting) — flagship performance variant
2. **H22A Blacktop** (200 PS, 10.6:1 CR, standard cams, no porting) — standard/premium variant

The Blacktop was positioned as the more accessible performance engine — still a DOHC VTEC with genuine performance credentials, but with a broader torque curve and lower maintenance sensitivity due to its more moderate valvetrain and lower compression ratio.

### Why Two Variants?

Honda offered both Redtop and Blacktop simultaneously in the JDM market to serve different buyer profiles:

- **Redtop buyers** wanted maximum high-RPM power (220 PS, 11.0:1 CR, 306°/310° cams) for track use and enthusiast driving
- **Blacktop buyers** wanted strong daily performance (200 PS, 10.6:1 CR, 288°/308° cams) with better low-end torque and wider service intervals

This dual-variant strategy was consistent with Honda's approach in the 1990s — offering both a "pure" performance variant and a more practical variant within the same engine family (similar to how the B-series offered B16A Type R and B16A/B18C variants).

## Market Exclusivity

### JDM — Exclusive Home Market

The H22A Blacktop was **JDM-exclusive** in its base form. Honda did not export the JDM-spec H22A Blacktop to any other market. Instead, each market received a locally calibrated variant:

| Market | Variant Code | Power | Key Differences |
|--------|-------------|-------|-----------------|
| **Japan (JDM)** | H22A (Blacktop) | 200 PS @ 6,800 RPM | Full JDM spec; 10.6:1 CR; P28/P06 ECU |
| **United States (USDM)** | H22A1 (4th gen) / H22A4 (5th gen) | 190–200 PS | Detuned for US fuel/emissions; OBD1/OBD2; different catalytic converters |
| **Europe (EDM)** | H22A2 (4th gen) / H22A5 (5th gen) | 185 PS | European emissions; 95 RON minimum fuel; different ECU mapping |
| **Australia (AUS)** | H22Z1 | 200 PS | RHD-compatible wiring; disputed power output (200–203 PS) |

### Non-Export Policy

Honda maintained a strict non-export policy for JDM-spec H22A engines during their production run. This meant:

- USDM Preludes received the detuned H22A1 (190 PS, 10.0:1 CR) or H22A4 (200 PS, 10.0:1 CR)
- European Preludes received the H22A2 (185 PS) or H22A5 (185 PS)
- Australian Preludes received the H22Z1 (200 PS, possibly up to 203 PS)

This policy contributed significantly to the Blacktop's status as a sought-after import engine in the grey market today.

## Key Differences from Redtop

| Feature | H22A Blacktop | H22A Redtop | Impact |
|---------|---------------|-------------|--------|
| Valve cover | Black | Red | Visual identification |
| Power | 200 PS @ 6,800 RPM | 220 PS @ 7,200 RPM | Redtop has +20 PS at higher RPM |
| Compression | 10.6:1 | 11.0:1 | Blacktop more tolerant of lower octane |
| Cams | 288°/308° | 306°/310° | Redtop has more aggressive profile |
| Lift | 11.38/10.54 mm | 11.91/11.12 mm | Redtop has +0.53/0.58 mm lift |
| Throttle body | 60 mm | 62.5 mm | Blacktop slightly restricted at high RPM |
| Exhaust | 51 mm | 57 mm | Blacktop more backpressure at high RPM |
| Intake manifold | Single runner (standard) | Dual runner (improved) | Redtop has better high-RPM airflow |
| Factory porting | No | Light | Redtop has mild CNC-style porting |
| Piston | Flat-top/small dome (-1.9 cc) | Dome (-1.8 cc) | Different combustion chamber geometry |
| Redline | 7,400 RPM (cutoff 7,600) | 8,200 RPM (cutoff 9,100) | Redtop designed for higher revving |

### Character Differences

The Blacktop's more moderate valvetrain and lower compression give it a **broader, more usable torque curve** compared to the Redtop's peaky, high-RPM character. The Blacktop is often described as:

- More tractable at low-to-mid RPM (better streetability)
- Slightly less responsive at high RPM (above 6,500 RPM)
- More tolerant of regular-grade fuel (91 RON vs 98 RON for Redtop)
- Easier to maintain long-term (less aggressive cam wear)

## Notes

### Conflicts and Disputes

1. **Accord SiR power output:** Multiple authoritative sources list the CD6 Accord SiR at 190 PS (Blacktop), while some sources (mywikimotors) list it at 220 PS (Redtop). Working hypothesis: production-year transition from Blacktop to Redtop during the CD6 run (early models 1994–1995 = Blacktop, later models 1996–1997 = Redtop). The block serial code ranges (H22A-2000001~ through H22A-2070001~) span both possibilities. **Requires verification against Honda service bulletins.**

2. **Mywikimotors power discrepancy for Blacktop:** mywikimotors lists the Blacktop at 190 HP @ 6,800 RPM in one section but 200 HP @ 7,000 RPM elsewhere. Wikipedia and QWEN.md consistently report 200 PS @ 6,800–7,000 RPM. Working assumption: mywikimotors conflated the Accord SiR (190 PS) with the Prelude SiR (200 PS) variant.

3. **VTEC engagement RPM:** Sources vary between 5,200 RPM and 5,800 RPM. The variation likely reflects different calibration between 4th gen and 5th gen ECUs. **Needs further verification.**

4. **Torneo Euro-R engine:** Some sources conflate the Torneo Euro-R with the Accord SiR. The Torneo received the **Redtop** (220 PS), not the Blacktop. This is an important distinction for variant identification.

### Identification Tips

- **Valve cover color** is the primary visual identifier: black = Blacktop, red = Redtop
- **Block serial codes** provide definitive identification (see tables above)
- **Throttle body size:** 60 mm = Blacktop, 62.5 mm = Redtop
- **Exhaust diameter:** 51 mm = Blacktop, 57 mm = Redtop
- **Camshaft duration:** 288°/308° = Blacktop, 306°/310° = Redtop
- **Note:** Valve covers are frequently repainted — always verify with block serial code or internal components
