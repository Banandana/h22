## Summary

The Honda H22A2-powered European Prelude BB1 was sold from 1993 to 1996 as the flagship variant of the 4th-generation Prelude range in Europe. Offered under the "2.2i VTEC" badging across two chassis configurations (BB1 4WS and BB4 2WS), it represented Honda's strategy to bring DOHC VTEC technology to European buyers at a competitive price point. The European lineup included three engine options — the H22A2 2.2i VTEC (185 PS), H23A 2.3i (160 PS SOHC non-VTEC), and B-series 2.0i (133 PS) — with the 2.2i VTEC commanding a premium over its siblings. Standard equipment was comprehensive for the era (ABS, dual airbags, power accessories, cruise control), while optional packages included 4WS with ATTS, leather interior, alloy wheels, and a moonroof. Indicative base pricing was approximately €30,800 in the early-to-mid 1990s European market.

### Key Takeaways

- The European Prelude 2.2i VTEC (H22A2) was the flagship trim of the 4th-gen Prelude range in Europe, produced 1993–1996.
- Three engine options were available: 2.0i (B-series, 133 PS), 2.3i (H23A SOHC, 160 PS), and 2.2i VTEC (H22A2, 185 PS).
- Two chassis configurations: BB1 (4WS) and BB4 (2WS), with 4WS being more popular in Europe than in the US.
- Standard equipment was comprehensive for the era: ABS, dual airbags (from 1994), power steering, power windows, cruise control, premium audio.
- Optional packages included 4WS with ATTS, leather interior, alloy wheels, moonroof, fog lights, and a 6-disc CD changer.
- Indicative base price was approximately €30,800; exact market-specific MSRPs were not located.
- The 2.2i VTEC commanded a premium over competitors (VW Corrado, Ford Probe GT, Toyota Celica GT-Four) justified by VTEC technology and build quality.
- The 1994 mid-cycle refresh brought significant interior and exterior updates: new center console, electro-luminescent cluster, LED spoiler brake light, lighter wheels, dual airbags standard.
