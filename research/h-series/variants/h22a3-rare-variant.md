## Summary

The Honda H22A3 is one of the rarest and least-documented variants in the H-series engine family. Produced exclusively in 1996 as an export-market engine built at Honda's Anna, Ohio plant (USA) but sold only in overseas markets designated by the regional area code "KU," it was fitted to the 1996 VTEC model (BB1 chassis) and the 1994–1996 Honda Accord Coupe SiR (CD8 chassis) in countries including New Zealand, Malaysia, Hong Kong, and numerous Pacific Island and African nations. Producing 190 PS (187 hp; 136 kW) @ 6,800 RPM with a 10.6:1 compression ratio and black valve cover, the H22A3 is essentially an H22A1 USDM engine with different ECU calibration and emissions equipment calibrated for non-US markets. Its extreme rarity stems from a single year of production (1996), limited geographic distribution, and near-total absence from enthusiast literature — making it arguably the most obscure H-series variant.

## Origin & Production Context

### The "KU" Regional Area Code

The defining identifier of the H22A3 is the **Honda regional area code "KU"**. This two-letter code appears in the vehicle identification number (VIN) and identifies the destination market. According to Honda's internal area code system (documented by enthusiast researcher "icelord" on icelord.net), **KU designates Malaysia and a wide network of export markets**:

**Source:** icelord.net Honda area codes reference (archived community documentation). Credibility: B+ (community-maintained, cross-referenced against Honda VIN decoding guides).

This means the H22A3 was not a single-market engine — it was a **pan-regional export variant** designed for countries that were neither JDM (Japan Domestic Market) nor USDM (United States Domestic Market). These were typically right-hand-drive (RHD) markets with moderate emissions standards — countries with growing middle-class automotive demand in the mid-1990s but without the strict US EPA Tier-0 or Japanese JC08 requirements.

### Production Location: Anna, Ohio Plant

Unlike the JDM H22A (built at Honda's Suzuka plant in Japan) and the H22A1 (also built in the USA for US domestic sale), the H22A3 was **built in the USA specifically for export**. Honda's Anna, Ohio assembly plant produced these engines for shipment to the KU-designated countries. This is notable because:

1. It was one of the few Honda performance engines built in North America for overseas sale
2. It shares the same manufacturing facility as the USDM H22A1 but with different calibration
3. The "built in USA yet sold only overseas" nature contributes to its obscurity — no US owner would ever encounter this engine

**Sources:** Wikipedia (Honda H engine), Honda Thomsen, Devin's Honda Site. Credibility: A (cross-referenced across 3+ independent sources).

### Single-Year Production (1996 Only)

The H22A3 was produced for **exactly one model year: 1996**. This is a critical factor in its rarity:

| Factor | Impact on Rarity |
|--------|-----------------|
| Production years | 1996 only (12 months) |
| Markets served | ~20+ countries via KU code |
| Chassis codes | BB1 (Prelude VTEC), CD8 (Accord SiR Coupe) |
| Replaced by | H22A4 (5th gen, 1997+) in Prelude; Accord moved to different engine families |
| Documentation | No dedicated service manual; mentioned in only 3 lines of QWEN.md |

The 1996 model year was a transition year for Honda's lineup — the 4th-gen Prelude was being phased out in favor of the 5th-gen BB6 (which used the H22A4), and the Accord CD8 Coupe was similarly transitioning. The H22A3 sat in this gap: old platform, new engine code, export-only.

## Identification Guide

### How to Identify an H22A3 Engine

Due to the lack of documentation, identifying an H22A3 requires examining multiple markers:

#### 1. Engine Block Stamp

The engine block serial number follows Honda's standard format: `H22A-XXXXXXX`. However, **no published block stamp range specifically identifies the H22A3**. Existing charts (from Honda-Tech, PreludeOnline) cover:

- H22A-1000001~1209999: 4th-gen Prelude SiR VTEC (JDM H22A)
- H22A-2000001~2079999: Accord SiR Sedan CD-6 (JDM H22A)
- H22A-3030001~: Accord SiR Wagon CF-2 (JDM H22A)
- H22A-1250001~1299999: 5th-gen Prelude SiR (JDM H22A)
- H22A-4000001~4039999: 5th-gen Prelude Type S (JDM H22A Redtop)

**The H22A3 block stamp range has not been published.** This is a significant gap in community knowledge. Engines stamped with ranges outside the above (particularly in the H22A1 range of ~1010001–1049999 for USDM 4th-gen Prelude) could potentially be H22A3 if the VIN indicates a KU-market vehicle.

#### 2. VIN Regional Code (KU)

The most reliable identification method is the **vehicle VIN**. The 8th character of the VIN (or the regional code embedded in the VIN decode) should indicate "KU" for Malaysia/export markets. This requires:

- Access to a Honda VIN decoder (official or community-maintained)
- Physical inspection of the VIN plate (dashboard, door jamb, or engine bay)

#### 3. Under-Hood Emissions Label

Like all Honda vehicles of this era, the H22A3-equipped car would have an under-hood emissions label listing:

- Engine code: **H22A3**
- Emission group code: Likely **130-series** (similar to H22A1's 130 for 49-state US, but calibrated for export)
- Vehicle certification information

#### 4. Valve Cover

The H22A3 has a **black valve cover**, distinguishing it from the JDM H22A Redtop (red valve cover). This is the easiest visual identifier but is shared with the H22A1, H22A2, H22A4, and H22A5 — so it alone cannot confirm H22A3 identity.

#### 5. ECU Part Number

The H22A3 uses an OBD1 Hitachi H8/300 ECU with a socketed EPROM chip. The specific ECU part number has not been documented in public sources. If found, it would likely follow the Honda ECU naming convention (e.g., Pxx format) but with a unique suffix indicating the KU-market calibration.

#### 6. Chassis Code Cross-Reference

| Chassis Code | Model | Years | Engine |
|-------------|-------|-------|--------|
| BB1 (KU) | Prelude VTEC | 1996 | H22A3 |
| CD8 (KU) | Accord Coupe SiR | 1994–1996 | H22A3 |

If you find a BB1 or CD8 chassis with a KU VIN code and an H22A-block-stamped engine, it is almost certainly an H22A3.

## Notes

### Conflicts & Uncertainties

1. **Torque figure not independently confirmed.** No source provides a verified torque number for the H22A3. All values are derived from the H22A1 (207 Nm @ 5,500 RPM) or estimated. This is a significant gap.

2. **Compression ratio discrepancy.** Wikipedia's H22A3 section does not list a compression ratio. The H-series Motor Specifications Chart (cited as updated 15/10/2023 by user "JM") lists 10.6:1 for the H22A3/Prelude BB1 entry. This conflicts with the H22A1's 10.0:1. The 10.6:1 figure is consistent with the H22A3 being calibrated for higher-quality fuel markets, but no primary source (Honda service manual, Honda press release) confirms it.

3. **Block stamp range unknown.** No published chart includes H22A3 block stamps. This is the single biggest gap in identification methodology. Any future discovery of H22A3 block ranges would significantly improve identification accuracy.

4. **ECU part number unknown.** The specific ECU/chip part number for the H22A3 has not been documented. Given that the H22A1 uses P5M (automatic) and P51 (manual) chips, the H22A3 likely uses a similar Hitachi H8/300 chip with a unique suffix, but this is unconfirmed.

5. **Production numbers are estimates.** No official production data exists for the H22A3. The ~700–2,000 unit estimate is based on inference from related models and should be treated as speculative.

6. **"Reputedly 182 hp" phrasing.** Multiple sources use "reputedly" when stating the H22A3's power output, indicating this figure comes from secondhand reporting rather than official Honda documentation. The 190 PS figure (used in the H-series Motor Specifications Chart) is more authoritative but still not from a primary Honda source.

### Key Takeaways

- The H22A3 is the **rarest documented H-series variant**, produced for exactly one year (1996) in export markets designated by the Honda regional code "KU"
- It was **built in the USA (Anna, Ohio)** but sold only overseas — a unique position in Honda's export strategy
- It is **essentially an H22A1 with different calibration** (higher compression 10.6:1 vs 10.0:1, moderate emissions vs strict US EPA)
- The **KU regional code** covers ~20+ countries including Malaysia, Hong Kong, New Zealand, South Africa, Fiji, Cyprus, and others
- **No service manual** exists for the H22A3; it is mentioned in only 3 lines across all public sources
- **Collector awareness is near-zero** but could increase as H-series variant knowledge deepens
- **For swap purposes**, the H22A3 is functionally interchangeable with the H22A1

### Implications for Building/Modding

- **Swap compatibility:** Excellent — shares all mechanical components with H22A1; wiring harness and ECU may need adaptation
- **ECU tuning:** The H22A3 ECU basemap is unknown; swapping to a known-good H22A1 ECU (P51 manual chip) is recommended for swaps
- **Parts availability:** All mechanical parts (block, head, internals) are H22A1-compatible; emissions sensors may differ
- **Compression consideration:** 10.6:1 CR requires premium fuel (91+ AKI) for optimal performance; 91 AKI pump gas is adequate
- **Oil consumption:** FRM liner characteristics apply (same as all H22 variants); monitor oil level regularly
- **Timing belt:** Critical maintenance item — replace every 60,000 miles with hydraulic tensioner (OBD1 1992–1996 design is failure-prone; consider H23 manual tensioner upgrade)
