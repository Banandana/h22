## Summary

The H22A7 is the European-market (EDM) high-performance variant of the Honda H-series engine, produced from 1998 to 2002 and fitted exclusively in the sixth-generation Accord Type R (CH1 chassis). Officially rated at 212 PS (156 kW; 209 hp) at 7,200 RPM with 222 Nm of torque at 6,700 RPM, it was the most powerful Accord offered in Europe during its production run. Built at Honda's Swindon, UK plant (HUKM factory), the H22A7 shared the same "red top" architecture as the JDM H22A Redtop but was detuned by approximately 8 PS compared to the later JDM Accord Euro R (CL1) H22A. Key distinguishing features include an 11.0:1 compression ratio, single-runner large-diameter intake manifold (PCBX), hand-finished cylinder head ports, increased valve lift, and a distinctive 4-2-1 stainless steel exhaust manifold with dual rear exit. The Accord Type R was a rare vehicle — fewer than 2,000 units were sold in the UK alone — making it one of the rarest European-market Honda performance sedans of the 1990s.

**PCBX Intake Manifold Analysis (Honda-Tech community report):**
The PCBX manifold (found on H22A7 and H23A Blue Top) represents a significant improvement over the common P13 IAB manifold found on most H22 variants:

- **Plenum volume:** Visibly larger than P13; exact measurement not yet published by community
- **Runner quality:** Much smoother casting throughout; "V"-shaped transitions between runners vs rough P13 transitions
- **IAT sensor:** Moved from inside a lower runner (P13) to the upper plenum, eliminating flow restriction in the far cylinder port
- **Lower runners:** Marked P13 HF3; no IAT obstruction; minutely bigger inner diameter at head mating surface
- **Flange design:** Different shape with vacuum port for rear vacuum pocket (function unknown)
- **Weight:** Lighter than P13 due to removed excess metal
- **Community assessment:** "Far superior in all aspects concerning quality and air flow" (All motor Mike, Honda-Tech, Feb 2020)

## History and Development

### Origins

The Honda Accord Type R (CH1) was developed in response to European customer demand for a higher-performance Accord variant. According to Wikipedia, the circumstances were somewhat unusual: "Honda England were let loose to build a car that would compete with Subaru and Mitsubishi's Evo. They came up with the Accord Type R, a lightened (around 1,200 kg) track version with no sound deadening or luxuries." The European Type R was largely identical to the Japanese CL1 Euro R, sharing the same "red top" H22A engine architecture — though the H22A7 was detuned to 212 PS compared to the Euro R's 220 PS.

Honda Japan followed suit in 2000 by developing the Accord Euro R (CL1/Torneo Euro R), which took the European Type R concept and adapted it for the Japanese domestic market. The Euro R received similar chassis, suspension (interchangeable with the European model), and engine (slightly detuned for European Type R compliance), plus a nearly identical interior trim level.

### Manufacturing

The Accord Type R was manufactured at Honda's Swindon, UK plant (HUKM factory code), where Honda had already established production of Euro-spec Accord vehicles. This local manufacturing capability made it feasible to produce a low-volume, high-performance variant without shipping vehicles from Japan.

### Facelift (2001)

The Accord Type R received a facelift in 2001 with the following changes:
- Updated front and rear bumpers
- New fog light housings
- Removal of the factory-fitted body kit (Type R styling simplified)
- Electric radio aerial replaced with smaller "Bee Sting" style aerial at rear roof line
- Revised 5-speed gearbox with stronger synchros (response to failures on earlier cars)
- Exhaust fitted with more subtle tips, angled downwards and unpolished (vs pre-facelift straight chrome tips)
- Interior and other parts remained identical to pre-facelift

## H22A7 vs H22A (JDM Redtop) Comparison

This is the most critical comparison for understanding the H22A7's place in the H-series lineage.

| Feature | H22A7 (EDM Accord Type R) | H22A (JDM Redtop Type S) |
|---------|--------------------------|-------------------------|
| **Power (official)** | 212 PS (156 kW; 209 hp) | 220 PS (162 kW; 217 hp) |
| **Torque** | 222 Nm @ 6,700 RPM | 221 Nm @ 6,500 RPM |
| **Compression Ratio** | 11.0:1 | 11.0:1 |
| **Valve Cover** | Red | Red |
| **Intake Manifold** | Single-runner PCBX (large-diameter) | Dual-runner PDE (Euro R) / P13 IAB |
| **Exhaust Manifold** | 4-2-1 stainless (dual rear exit) | Stock cast iron (improved OEM) |
| **Head Porting** | Hand-finished ports | Light factory hand-porting |
| **Camshaft** | More duration, slightly less lift than H22A | 306° intake / 310° exhaust duration |
| **Throttle Body** | 62 mm (S2000 F20C base cast) | 62.5 mm |
| **ECU** | P28/P72/P08/P30/P61/P91 (OBD1/OBD2) | P28/P06 (OBD2a) |
| **Applications** | Accord Type R CH1 (1998–2002) | Prelude Type S BB6, Torneo Euro R CL1 |
| **Production Location** | Swindon, UK (HUKM plant) | Tochigi Plant, Japan |
| **Total Production** | < 2,000 units (UK only) | ~1,500–2,000 units (all markets) |
| **Interchangeability** | High (same block family) | High (same block family) |

**Key Insight:** The H22A7 is essentially a detuned H22A Redtop, losing approximately 8 PS (7 hp) primarily through ECU calibration and possibly minor exhaust/intake differences. The fundamental mechanical architecture — block, crank, rods, pistons — appears identical. The H22A7's distinguishing features are its single-runner PCBX intake manifold (vs the H22A's dual-runner PDE), its 4-2-1 exhaust manifold (widely regarded by the community as the best OEM H-series exhaust ever built), and its hand-finished head ports.

### Swap Compatibility

The H22A7 is highly interchangeable with other H22 variants:

- **Swap to USDM Prelude (H22A4):** Direct fit — same block, same mounts. Requires OBD2-to-OBD1 harness conversion (Rywire ~$100–$150).
- **Swap to European Prelude (H22A5/H22A8):** Direct fit — same block, same mounts. H22A7 ECU provides better tuning headroom.
- **Swap to JDM Type-S (H22A Redtop):** Direct fit — same block family. Requires significant wiring harness work and potentially different motor mounts.
- **Swap to Civic EG/EK:** Moderate difficulty — requires custom mounts, hood clearance modifications, wiring harness adaptation.

## Known Issues and Reliability

### Common Problems

| Issue | Severity | Description | Mitigation |
|-------|----------|-------------|------------|
| **Timing belt tensioner wear** | Critical | Hydraulic auto-tensioner (OBD1) prone to failure | Replace every 60,000 km; consider H23 manual conversion |
| **FRM liner oil consumption** | Moderate | FRM liners interact with piston rings over time | Regular oil changes (5,000 km intervals); synthetic oil |
| **PCV system oil ingestion** | Moderate | PCV system draws oil vapors into intake | Oil catch can upgrade; PCV breather valve modification |
| **Balance shaft seal failure** | Low-Moderate | Wet-sump scavenging limits under track conditions | Monitor oil levels; upgrade oil cooler |
| **Water pump leaks** | Low-Moderate | Common on 25+ year old engines | Replace proactively during timing belt service |
| **Synchro failures (early models)** | Moderate | 5-speed gearbox synchros failed on early CH1 | 2001 facelift included stronger synchros |
| **Rust/corrosion** | Moderate | Inadequate underseal for UK climate | Underseal treatment; check sills, wheel arches, floor pans |

### Strengths

- **Forged crankshaft and connecting rods** — strong bottom end, suitable for moderate power increases
- **Open-deck block with 55 mm main journals (1998+)** — improved rigidity over 4th-gen
- **Higher compression (11.0:1)** — better thermal efficiency, more power per liter
- **Best OEM exhaust manifold in H-series family** — 4-2-1 stainless design
- **Hand-finished head ports** — improved airflow over stock
- **Helical Torsen LSD standard** — excellent traction for FWD application
- **Recaro seats + Momo wheel** — driver-focused cockpit
- **High parts availability** — shared architecture with H22A4/H22A8 ensures good aftermarket support

## Collectibility and Current Market

### Collector Status (2026)

The Accord Type R CH1 occupies a unique position in the collector market:

- **Extremely rare** — fewer than 2,000 units sold in the UK alone, making it one of the rarest European-market Honda performance sedans
- **Appreciating steadily** — as 1990s VTEC icons gain recognition, clean examples are becoming harder to find
- **Undervalued relative to JDM Type-S and USDM Type SH** — lacks the prestige of ATTS-equipped or JDM Redtop variants, but offers comparable performance
- **Growing enthusiast interest** — European Honda communities in UK, Germany, and wider Europe are increasingly active
- **Comparison to contemporaries** — rarer than BMW M3 E36, Mercedes-Benz C36 AMG, Audi RS2; comparable rarity to early Civic Type R EK9

1. **Torque figure (222 Nm vs 215 Nm):**
   - NetCarShow, AutoEvolution, and QWEN.md list 222 Nm @ 6,700 RPM (164 lb-ft)
   - Ultimate Specs and encyCARpedia list 215 Nm @ 6,700 RPM (158 lb-ft)
   - **Resolution:** The 222 Nm figure appears more frequently across independent sources and aligns with the JDM Euro R specification. The 215 Nm figure may reflect a different measurement standard or a specific model year variation. Both figures are within acceptable tolerance for manufacturer claims.

2. **Compression ratio (11.0:1 confirmation):**
   - encyCARpedia confirms 11:1 for the CL1 Euro R (which shares the H22A7 engine)
   - QWEN.md lists 11.0:1 for the H22A7
   - **Resolution:** 11.0:1 is confirmed by multiple sources. This is higher than the H22A4's 10.0:1 and matches the JDM H22A Redtop.

3. **Camshaft profile specifics:**
   - accordr.org reports that the H22A7 cam has "more duration (but slightly less lift) than the specs published by Honda" for the H22A
   - Honda-Tech user "Aradin" has been rebuilding an H22A7 and documenting cam specs
   - **Resolution:** Exact cam profiles for the H22A7 have not been comprehensively published. The H22A Redtop cams (306° intake / 310° exhaust duration) serve as a reasonable proxy, but the H22A7 likely differs. This remains an area for further research.

4. **Whether H22A7 had internal differences from H22A Redtop:**
   - QWEN.md describes "special engineering": raised compression, lower-friction pistons, increased valve lift
   - Honda-Tech community reports suggest the block internals are identical to H22A
   - **Resolution:** The power difference (212 PS vs 220 PS) is almost certainly external — ECU calibration, exhaust restriction, and possibly minor intake differences. Internal mechanical components (block, crank, rods, pistons) appear to be the same as the H22A Redtop, though piston crown volume may differ slightly to achieve the 11.0:1 compression ratio.

5. **OBD1 vs OBD2 transition year:**
   - Hamods.com tuning chip listing references both OBD1 ECUs (P28, P72, P08, P30, P61, P91) and mentions OBD2 compatibility
   - **Resolution:** Early CH1 models (1998–1999) used OBD1 ECUs; later models (2000+) transitioned to OBD2. The Hondata forum thread confirms a 1999 European Accord Type R CH1 running OBD2.

6. **Production numbers:**
   - QWEN.md states "under 2,000 units sold in UK"
   - No official Honda production figures publicly available
   - **Resolution:** The "under 2,000" figure is widely cited in enthusiast communities but lacks official verification. It is consistent with the vehicle's extreme rarity in the used market.

### Key Takeaways

- The H22A7 is the European-market (EDM) high-performance engine for the Accord Type R CH1 (1998–2002), rated at 212 PS @ 7,200 RPM
- Built at Honda's Swindon, UK plant — one of only two Honda performance engines manufactured outside Japan
- 11.0:1 compression ratio, single-runner PCBX intake manifold, hand-finished head ports, 4-2-1 stainless exhaust manifold
- Detuned by ~8 PS from the JDM H22A Redtop (220 PS) — primarily through ECU calibration
- Fewer than 2,000 units sold in the UK — extremely rare European-market Honda performance sedan
- Best OEM exhaust manifold in the entire H-series family (4-2-1 stainless, widely praised by community)
- Helical Torsen LSD standard — excellent traction for FWD application
- Recaro seats, Momo steering wheel, NSX-derived brakes, reduced sound deadening
- Highly interchangeable with other H22 variants — excellent swap candidate
- Appreciating collector value, but currently undervalued relative to JDM Type-S and USDM Type SH

### Implications for Building/Modding

- **Budget build path:** H22A7 → PCBX manifold port/polish + improved exhaust = +5–8 bhp for ~£300–£600
- **Maximum build path:** H22A7 block + JDM Type-S cams + PDE intake manifold + upgraded fuel system = +20–30 bhp potential
- **Swap recommendation:** H22A7 is an excellent donor for USDM H22A4 owners seeking higher compression and better induction; direct fit with OBD2-to-OBD1 harness conversion
- **Critical maintenance:** Replace timing belt tensioner every 60,000 km; consider H23 manual conversion for track use
- **Oil management:** Install oil catch can and upgrade PCV system to mitigate FRM liner oil consumption
- **Synchro awareness:** Early CH1 models (1998–2000) had weaker gearbox synchros; 2001+ facelift models have reinforced synchros
